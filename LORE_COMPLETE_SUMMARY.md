## MAJOR FACTIONS

### 1. THE HOLY DOMINION (Human-Elven Empire)
- **Type**: Theocratic imperial state
- **Population**: 1,500,000 (humans + elves)
- **Territory**: Central continent, most developed regions
- **Leader**: The Emperor (unnamed)
- **Deity**: Helios, the Sun God
- **Doctrine**: "Magic is not a right. It is a weapon."
- **Enforcement**: The Luminary Inquest
- **Status**: Dominant continental power

**Key Features**:
- Claims divine mandate from Helios
- Won Last World War using Heaven's Atlas
- Declared magic illegal unless state-sanctioned or divinely ordained
- Fully integrated elves as bureaucratic caste after destroying their homeland
- Controls trade, documentation, military, magic licensing

### 2. THE FREE HOLDS OF STONE (Dwarves)
- **Type**: Collectivist labor federation
- **Population**: 185,000
- **Territory**: Northern mountain underground holds
- **Leader**: Guild Councils (rotating)
- **Philosophy**: "Labor belongs to those who give it."
- **Status**: Independent, isolationist

**Key Features**:
- Anarcho-syndicalist society with no hierarchy
- Stone-born reproduction (no biological parents)
- Guild councils, collective ownership, labor rotation
- Refuse imperial authority completely
- Never been successfully invaded

### 3. THE ORC CLANS
- **Type**: Nomadic military confederation (fragmented)
- **Population**: 240,000
- **Territory**: Western steppes
- **Leader**: None (no current Khan)
- **Philosophy**: "Merit over blood. Loyalty to Khan. Law applies to all."
- **Status**: Dormant superpower

**Key Features**:
- Once most effective military force in history
- Fragmented after Great Khan's death
- Preserve laws, routes, commands through oral tradition
- Imperial fear: "They only need to unite again."
- Warfare doctrine: psychological collapse, not attrition

### 4. THE GNOMISH COLLECTIVE
- **Type**: Collectivist industrial state
- **Population**: 340,000
- **Territory**: Eastern island continent (Gnomish Isles)
- **Leader**: Production Councils
- **Philosophy**: "Gnomes do not believe in ownership. They believe in function."
- **Status**: Independent, secretive, isolated

**Key Features**:
- Absolute collective ownership, no private property
- Advanced automation (population multipliers)
- Secret airship fleets
- No religion (faith is personal curiosity)
- No successful invasion ever
- "Secrecy is class defense"

### 5. THE SHADOW FEN COMMUNE
- **Type**: Magically concealed anarchist commune
- **Population**: 8,000-12,000
- **Territory**: Southwestern swamplands
- **Leader**: Secret Council (membership unknown)
- **Philosophy**: "We chose devils over tyrants. At least devils are honest."
- **Status**: De facto independent, de jure denied

**Key Features**:
- Founded by refugees fleeing imperial control
- Protected by pervasive magical Veil (concealment field)
- Infernal pacts harden borders (devils/demons provide protection)
- Communal labor, burned identities, past lives don't matter
- Houses The Veiled Hand assassin organization
- Empire tolerates as containment zone

### 6. THE GOBLIN RESISTANCE
- **Type**: Decentralized guerrilla network
- **Population**: Unknown
- **Territory**: Scattered (tunnels, ruins, warrens)
- **Leader**: None (cell-based)
- **Philosophy**: "What was taken will be remembered. What is remembered cannot be erased."
- **Status**: Persistent insurgency

**Key Features**:
- Driven from ancestral lands during imperial expansion
- Cell-based organization (5-20 per cell)
- Asymmetric warfare: sabotage, ambush, economic attrition
- "Every warren cleared spawns two more"
- Empire calls it "pest control," reality: self-sustaining resistance

### 7. THE LIZARD FOLK SECTS
- **Type**: Secretive knowledge confederation
- **Population**: ~15,000
- **Territory**: Hidden rivers beneath northern deserts
- **Leader**: Sect councils (multiple)
- **Philosophy**: "What is hidden endures. What is revealed can be taken."
- **Status**: Hidden civilization

**Key Features**:
- Ancient hidden river empire still exists beneath sands
- Society divided into sects (River Engineering, Burial Rites, Trade Routes, Astronomy, Martial Discipline)
- 600-800 year lifespans (personally remember events from centuries ago)
- Founded The Veiled Hand after witnessing Calidar
- Information shared deliberately and incompletely

### 8. THE ELVEN ADMINISTRATION
- **Type**: Integrated bureaucratic caste
- **Population**: 500,000
- **Territory**: Southern Holy Dominion (former Calidar region)
- **Leader**: None (integrated into imperial structure)
- **Philosophy**: "What is written endures. What is remembered survives."
- **Status**: Junior partners in Holy Dominion

**Key Features**:
- Homeland (Calidar) destroyed 500 years ago by Heaven's Atlas
- 80-90% of population killed
- Integration was survival after genocide, not choice
- Serve as empire's bureaucratic backbone
- "Elves did not destroy magic. They hid it." (sealed archives)
- Old Ones (500+) personally witnessed destruction

### 9. THE VEILED HAND
- **Type**: Secretive assassination network
- **Population**: 200-500 operatives (estimated)
- **Territory**: Shadow Fen (base), Continental (operations)
- **Leader**: Unknown (highest initiates are lizard folk)
- **Philosophy**: "Preventing a single decision can save countless lives."
- **Status**: Rumored to exist, officially denied

**Key Features**:
- Founded by lizard folk sect after witnessing Calidar
- Exists to prevent escalation to mass atrocity
- Targets: Officials authorizing mass purges, developing WMDs, planning invasions
- Methods: Precision removal through staged accidents, poison, natural-appearing deaths
- Compartmentalized cells, no member knows full scope
- Operates from Shadow Fen, protects commune

### 10. BEAST FOLK (Cat Folk Diaspora)
- **Type**: Stateless diaspora culture
- **Population**: 45,200 (30,000 cat folk)
- **Territory**: None (scattered across all lands)
- **Leader**: None (extended family groups)
- **Philosophy**: "Beast folk do not recognize borders as permanent truths."
- **Status**: Diasporic, marginal, heavily scrutinized

**Key Features**:
- Displaced from desert homelands by war, drought, expansion
- Travel in family groups along established routes
- Cat folk specialize in gambling (pattern recognition, not magic)
- Parallel legal systems (internal mediation)
- Heaviest Luminary Inquest scrutiny
- "Tolerated, distrusted, romanticized, scapegoated—often at the same time"

## CENTRAL CONFLICTS

### 1. Imperial Control vs Regional Independence
- Holy Dominion attempts to extend authority
- Dwarves refuse (mountains), Gnomes refuse (ocean), Orcs refuse (mobility)
- Stalemate: Empire controls what it can, tolerates what it cannot eliminate

### 2. Magic Regulation vs Unsanctioned Practice
- Empire: "Magic is not a right. It is a weapon."
- Shadow Fen: Practices openly in hidden refuge
- Elves: Hide it in sealed archives
- Result: Luminary Inquest hunts fugitives, some escape to fen

### 3. The Imperial Documentation System: Bureaucracy as Weapon
The Holy Dominion requires all beings within its claimed borders to carry official papers at all times. These documents record identity, legal status, magical lineage, travel history, economic activity, and religious standing. Papers must be presented at city gates, bridge crossings, provincial borders, during random inspections, when demanded by the Luminary Inquest, and when entering any imperial facility.

The system exists for control, not safety.

| Race | Relationship to the System |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Humans** | Accept it as normal. "Papers keep us safe." Most citizens view documentation as reasonable infrastructure. |
| **Elves** | Process the papers AND carry them. Elves are the bureaucrats who stamp, file, and maintain the records. They know every loophole. Some quietly help others forge documents or make records disappear. |
| **Orcs** | Comply with visible resentment. Enhanced documentation, two-year renewal, targeted at checkpoints at rates far exceeding other races. Carrying papers proving the right to exist on ancestral land is constant humiliation. |
| **Beast Folk** | Hit hardest. As nomadic peoples, their travel history is always suspect. Stopped more often, papers "lost" by authorities, must re-register in every city entered. Some carry multiple copies knowing documents will be confiscated. |
| **Goblins** | REFUSE ENTIRELY. Do not carry papers, do not recognize imperial authority. Lacking papers is a criminal offense, making every goblin a criminal from birth. This is by design. |
| **Lizard Folk** | Minimal compliance when traveling imperial lands. Sect secrecy means they reveal as little as possible. Many in the Hidden River Territories have no papers at all. |
| **Dwarves** | Carry minimal documentation at surface trade posts. Bristle at every demand. Inside their holds, no such system exists or is recognized. |
| **Gnomes** | Encounter the system only at Clockwork Harbor, where they control the interaction. View it as evidence of human paranoia. |
| **Shadow Fen** | Papers are burned upon entry. The commune exists outside the documentation system entirely. |

The system is administered primarily by elven bureaucrats, creating the central irony that the surveillance apparatus designed to control non-humans is operated by non-humans who know every weakness in the system they maintain.

### 4. Memory as Resistance vs Imperial Forgetting
- Elves: Remember Calidar. Document everything. Prepare for next empire.
- Goblins: Remember lost homelands. Teach children names. Outlast empires.
- Lizard Folk: 600-800 year lifespans. Personal memory spans centuries.
- Empire: "The past is settled. Order matters now."

### 5. Escalation vs Deterrence
- Hardliners: Expand purges, develop new weapons, plan invasions
- Veiled Hand: Remove those who authorize escalation before they act
- Moderates: Exist in both empire and resistance
- Cycle: Escalation → removal → new hardliner → repeat

## THEMATIC ELEMENTS

### Memory and Forgetting
- **Elves**: Document everything. "What is written endures."
- **Goblins**: Oral tradition. "What is remembered cannot be erased."
- **Lizard Folk**: Centuries-long memory. Witness patterns others miss.
- **Humans**: Humans cycle through memory every two or three generations, which means the empire is perpetually administered by people who were not there. The evidence of what they repeat is in every peace treaty that lasted sixty years.

### Survival Through Adaptation

Every major non-human faction adapted to imperial power the same way: they found the one thing the empire could not take from them and built their identity around it. Elves took their bureaucratic precision. Beast folk took their roads. Goblins took their refusal to disappear. Shadow Fen took their desperation and turned it into a theology. The empire took their lands, their magic, their political autonomy. It could not take what they became in response.

### Control and Resistance
- **Empire**: Control through documentation, enforcement, divine mandate
- **Dwarves/Gnomes**: Independence through isolation and self-sufficiency
- **Orcs**: Independence through mobility and dormant military threat
- **Shadow Fen/Goblins**: Resistance through concealment and asymmetry

### The Price of Power
- **Heaven's Atlas**: Destroyed Calidar, justified eternal magic ban
- **Infernal Pacts**: Protect Shadow Fen but corrupt residents
- **Integration**: Elves survived but serve destroyers
- **Luminary Inquest**: Order through terror, compliance through fear
