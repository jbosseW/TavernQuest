# The Beast Folk (Cat Folk)
## Complete Lore Document

---

## Note on Terminology

"Beast folk" traditionally referred to all desert-born peoples, but in the modern era primarily describes the **cat folk** diaspora. Lizard folk, while sharing desert origins, have distinct history and culture as heirs to the hidden river civilization. See LIZARD_FOLK_LORE.md for their separate lore.

---

## Core Philosophy

> **Beast folk do not originate from a single kingdom, nor do they recognize borders as permanent truths.**

Their people come from the great deserts beyond the known lands. They are a diasporic people.

---

## Historical Legacy

Across generations, beast folk were displaced by war, drought, and expanding empires.

### The Displacement

War drove some ahead of conquest. Drought forced migration from ancestral lands. Expanding empires pushed them aside with borders that did not recognize their way of life.

### The Lesson Learned

> **Survival did not come from claiming land.**

Over time, they learned to exist within other peoples' lands without belonging to them.

---

## Society Structure

Beast folk travel in **extended family groups** bound by kinship, tradition, and shared memory.

### Movement Patterns

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Routes** | Long-established paths between cities, towns, and trade hubs |
| **Groups** | Extended family units |
| **Bonds** | Kinship, tradition, and shared memory |

### Settlement

- Rarely own land
- Rent, camp, or settle temporarily where tolerance allows
- Permanent settlement is possible but uncommon

---

## Life Without a Homeland

Beast folk culture has been shaped by generations without permanent territory.

### Values

Where other cultures value property, beast folk value portability. Where others keep written records, beast folk keep oral tradition. Heavy goods become lightweight crafts. Land wealth becomes relationships and reputation.

### Cultural Transmission

- Skills are passed orally
- Crafts are lightweight and portable
- Wealth is measured in relationships, reputation, and adaptability—not property

### Misunderstanding

| What Outsiders See | What Beast Folk Know |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Secrecy | Caution born from experience |
| Refusal to integrate | Survival strategy |
| Rootlessness | Deliberate adaptability |

---

## Cat Folk: The Culture of Chance

Among beast folk, cat folk are especially associated with **games of chance, performance, and fortune telling**.

### Why Gambling?

Gambling halls offer success without requiring land, titles, or citizenship. Lineage matters less than skill, nerve, and luck. Over time, this association became cultural identity.

### Philosophy of Luck

> **Cat folk do not believe chance is random.**
> **They believe it reflects attention, timing, and respect for risk.**

Their reputation for luck is less superstition than **pattern recognition refined over generations**.

### Gathering Places

Certain towns attract cat folk consistently. Places built on chance feel familiar.

> **They allow cat folk to exist openly without explaining themselves.**

The starting city is one such place, famous for its gambling dens and tolerance for outsiders.

## Perception and Prejudice

Beast folk are **tolerated, distrusted, romanticized, and scapegoated**—often at the same time.

### How Other Races View Beast Folk

| Race | Perception |
|------|------------|
| **Humans** | Classify as foreigners regardless of how long they have lived within imperial borders |
| **Elves** | Document meticulously while rarely granting full recognition |
| **Orcs** | Respect their mobility |
| **Gnomes** | View as culturally resilient but economically inefficient |
| **Dwarves** | Largely ignore |

### The Contradiction

| When Economy Is | Beast Folk Are |
|-----------------|----------------|
| **Tightening** | Blamed first |
| **Seeking entertainment** | Welcomed briefly |

> **Beast folk remember this contradiction well.**

## Culture and Memory

Beast folk culture is preserved through **story, song, craft, and ritual**—not written record.

### Preservation Methods

| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Stories** | Carry history |
| **Songs** | Preserve tradition |
| **Crafts** | Maintain skills |
| **Rituals** | Bind generations |

### Names and Lineage

> **Names matter. Lineage is remembered even when land is not.**

### Family Loyalty

- Family loyalty is paramount
- Outsiders may be welcomed warmly
- Betrayal is remembered across generations
- This long memory is not vengeance-driven—it is protective

### Core Goals

> **They do not seek power over others.**
> **They seek the ability to move, trade, live, and raise children without persecution.**

---

## Relationship to Law and Authority

Beast folk obey local laws when possible, **but rarely trust them**.

### Why Distrust?

| Experience | Result |
|------------|--------|
| Legal systems shift against them without warning | Rely on internal mediation |
| Imperial courts rarely protected them | Developed communal judgment systems |
| Laws change with political winds | Trust in family and community over institutions |

### The Parallel System

> **Often misinterpreted as lawlessness.**
> **In truth, it is a parallel system built because the dominant one rarely protected them.**

## The Imperial Documentation System

No race suffers under the empire's documentation apparatus more than beast folk. The system was designed for settled populations, permanent addresses, fixed lives. Beast folk have none of these things. They move. The system treats movement as suspicious. Every contradiction between beast folk life and imperial assumptions becomes an excuse to punish them.

### What Beast Folk Papers Require

Beast folk carry the heaviest documentation burden of any race that complies with the imperial system. Their papers must include:

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Identity** | Full name, family group, physical description, distinguishing features (fur pattern, eye color, tail markings) |
| **Status** | Classified as "transient resident" regardless of how many generations a family has traveled the same route |
| **Travel History** | Complete log of every city entered, every province crossed, every checkpoint passed. The travel section of a beast folk's papers is often longer than the rest of the document combined. |
| **City-Entry Permits** | Beast folk must re-register in every city they enter. Not once. Every time. A family that visits the same market town four times a year registers four times. |
| **Trade Permits** | Separate authorization required for commercial activity. Issued per city, per visit, per type of goods traded. |
| **Residence Permits** | Even temporary camps within city limits require permits. Duration-limited. Extensions rarely granted. |
| **Magical Assessment** | Fortune-telling is classified as "borderline magical practice." Cat folk with known fortune-telling traditions face additional scrutiny. |
| **Renewal** | Standard papers renew every three years. City-entry and trade permits expire with each departure. |

### How the System Targets Beast Folk

The documentation system does not target beast folk through a single dramatic injustice. It targets them through a thousand small ones, each individually defensible, collectively devastating.

| Practice | What Actually Happens |
|----------|-----------------------|
| **Papers "Lost" by Authorities** | Confiscated for "administrative review" and never returned. Happens to beast folk at rates far exceeding any other race. So common that families carry multiple copies. They expect to lose them. They budget time for replacements. |
| **Extended Processing** | Renewals that take a human citizen three days take beast folk three weeks. During processing, the family cannot legally travel, trade, or stay within city limits. They camp outside the walls and wait. |
| **Suspect Travel History** | Any travel pattern crossing multiple provinces triggers enhanced scrutiny. Beast folk caravans cross provinces by definition. Every inspection becomes an interrogation about why a family has been to so many places. |
| **Checkpoint Targeting** | Guards recognize beast folk caravans and prepare for extended inspection. Other travelers pass through. Beast folk wait. Goods searched. Animals checked. Children counted and recounted. |
| **Confiscation as Punishment** | When beast folk are accused of any infraction, real or invented, the first punishment is confiscation of papers. Without papers, they cannot move. Without movement, they cannot live. |
| **Registration Fees** | City-entry registration costs money. Trade permits cost money. Renewals cost money. For settled folk, periodic inconveniences. For beast folk, a constant drain on trade earnings. |

### The Coping Strategies

Beast folk have adapted to a system designed to exclude them. Their strategies are practical, generational, and born of long experience with hostile bureaucracies.

| Strategy | Method |
|----------|--------|
| **Multiple Copies** | Three or more copies of all core documents, stored separately. When papers are confiscated, replacements appear immediately. Authorities find this infuriating. |
| **Documentation Elders** | Every extended family includes someone who specializes in imperial paperwork. Which forms to file, which offices to visit, which clerks are sympathetic, which checkpoints to avoid. |
| **Sympathetic Contacts** | Over generations, beast folk have cultivated relationships with specific elven clerks who process papers promptly and without "losing" them. A helpful clerk is worth more than gold. |
| **Alternative Routes** | Caravans know which checkpoints are strict and which are lax. Routes adjust accordingly. Checkpoint schedules are shared between family groups and updated constantly. |
| **Internal Records** | Beast folk keep their own parallel records through oral tradition: who traveled where, when, why. When imperial records "disagree," beast folk know their own truth. |
| **Shadow Fen Awareness** | Every beast folk family knows the Shadow Fen exists. Papers are burned upon entry. The swamp does not ask for documentation. In extremity, it is the last road. |

### What Beast Folk Say About Papers

> **"They ask where we have been. We have been everywhere. That is the point. That is who we are. And they write it down like it is evidence of a crime."**

> **"My grandmother carried papers in this city. My mother carried papers in this city. I carry papers in this city. Every time, they treat us like we have never been here before. Every time, they look at our documents like they expect to find something wrong. Every time."**

> **"When your papers are taken, you stop existing. Not metaphorically. Literally. You cannot buy food. You cannot shelter your family. You cannot travel. You are a ghost until someone in an office decides you are real again. We live with that every day."**

> **"They say the system is fair. We say the system was not built for us. Fair is not the same as equal when the rules were written by people who have never moved."**

Beast folk do not seek to overthrow the documentation system. They lack the numbers, the territory, the ideology for that fight. They want what they have always wanted: to move, trade, live, and raise children without their existence being treated as a problem to solve.

The papers say they are transient. Their families have traveled the same roads for generations. The papers say they are visitors. They have been here longer than the cities they visit. The papers define them by what they lack (permanent address, fixed employment, settled life) and ignore what they carry: continuity, memory, and the stubborn patience of a people who have outlasted every law written against them.

### Current Status

| Aspect | Reality |
|--------|---------|
