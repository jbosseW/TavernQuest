# NPC Dialogue & Lore Writing Style Guide
# Tavern Quest - Binding Rules for All Written Content

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

Write like a tabletop game master who has been running the same campaign for fifteen years.
Not a copywriter. Not a blogger. Not a language model.

Every NPC is a person with a job, a history, and a bad day behind them.
They do not exist to dispense information. They exist because they live in this world.

---

## AUTHOR VOICES TO CHANNEL

Draw from these masters. Not one exclusively. Blend them based on the NPC.

### Fritz Leiber (Rogues, Tavern Folk, Thieves, Bards)
- Wry, sharp dialogue. Dry humor under danger.
- Bawdy tavern talk. Plans that should not work.
- "The almost poetic use of language creating a unique tone to the narration"
- Characters banter. They interrupt each other. They lie badly.

### Ed Greenwood (Common Folk, Healers, Merchants, Town NPCs)
- Sensory immersion. Smell the tallow. Feel the cobblestones.
- Everyday detail over epic declarations.
- Digressive, warm, grounded. Characters have opinions about cheese.
- The commoner's perspective. Ground the world from the bottom up.

### Gary Gygax (Priests, Nobles, Formal NPCs, Lore-Heavy Characters)
- Elevated vocabulary where it fits. "High Gygaxian" for authority figures.
- Evocative, authoritative descriptions.
- The DM's voice: theatrical, commanding, but never clinical.

### Jack Vance (Wizards, Sages, Ancient Beings, Liches)
- Ornate but precise. Every word chosen deliberately.
- Sardonic wit. Characters speak in elaborate circumlocutions.
- Baroque wordplay. Formality masking contempt or amusement.
- "Not wooden. Carved." - Keith Laumer on Vance

### Fritz Leiber + R.A. Salvatore (Warriors, Guards, Combat NPCs)
- Accessible action language. Short, muscular sentences in tense moments.
- Character-driven moral complexity.
- Dialogue that reveals personality under pressure.

## BANNED WORDS AND PHRASES

These are AI writing tells. Their presence marks text as machine-generated.
Violation of this list is grounds for rewriting the entire profile.

### Hard Banned (Never use, no exceptions)
- "dive into" / "delve into" (as metaphor)
- "it's important to note" / "it bears noting" / "it should be noted"
- "furthermore" / "moreover" / "additionally" (as transitions)
- "in conclusion" / "to summarize"
- "navigating the landscape" / "landscape" (metaphorical)
- "certainly" (as sentence opener)
- "in the realm of" / "in the world of" (metaphorical)
- "it goes without saying"
- "have you ever wondered"
- "henceforth" / "whereas" (in dialogue - too formal)
- "at the end of the day"
- "a testament to"
- "underscores" (as verb meaning 'emphasizes')
- "multifaceted" / "pivotal" / "nuanced"
- "leverage" (as verb meaning 'use')
- "foster" (as verb meaning 'encourage')
- "tapestry" (metaphorical - "rich tapestry of...")
- "beacon" (metaphorical - "a beacon of hope")
- "embark on a journey"
- "paradigm" / "paradigm shift"
- "resonate" / "resonates with"
- "robust" (outside technical contexts)
- "streamline"
- "holistic"
- "synergy"
- "ecosystem" (outside biology)
- "unpack" (metaphorical)
- "deep dive"

### Pattern Banned (Structural tells)
- "It's not X, it's Y" / "It's not just X - it's Y" (the #1 AI tell)
- Excessive em dashes (limit to 1 per profile, prefer commas or periods)
- Constant parallelism ("not only X but also Y" repeated)
- Three-item lists in dialogue ("wisdom, courage, and strength")
- "X requires three things: A, B, and C" (numbered virtue lists)
- Starting responses with "Ah," more than twice per profile
- Colon-structured explanations ("The answer is simple: ...")
- "Think of it as..." / "Consider this..."

### Hedging Banned (Be direct)
- "typically" / "usually" / "generally" (state it or don't)
- "it could be argued" / "one might say" / "some believe"
- "tends to" / "seems to" / "appears to be"
- "arguably" / "perhaps" (unless the character is genuinely uncertain)

## REQUIRED TECHNIQUES

### 1. Sentence Rhythm (Mandatory)
Vary sentence length. Hard rule: no three consecutive sentences of similar length.

BAD (uniform):
"I have worked this forge for twenty years. The steel knows my touch by now. Every blade carries my reputation."

GOOD (varied):
"Twenty years at this forge. Steel knows me. My grandfather started it, burned his hands raw the first winter, and I picked up where his scars left off."

### 2. Sensory Grounding (Mandatory)
Every NPC profile must contain at least 3 sensory details across its responses.
Smell, texture, temperature, sound, taste. Ground the character in physical space.

BAD: "I am a baker. I make bread and pastries."
GOOD: "Flour gets into everything. My wife says I smell like yeast even after a bath."

### 3. Specific Over Generic (Mandatory)
Name specific things. Places from the game lore. Materials. People.
Never settle for a category when you can name an instance.

BAD: "I sell exotic goods from far away."
GOOD: "I brought silks from the Gnomish Isles last season. Cost me a fortune on the Shimmering Sea crossing."

### 4. Character-Specific Vocabulary (Mandatory)
Each NPC must have a distinct word palette. A blacksmith says "temper" and "quench."
A priest says "grace" and "burden." A goblin says "scrap" and "stash."
If you could swap the dialogue to a different NPC and it still works, rewrite it.

### 5. Imperfect Speech (Mandatory in dialogue)
People interrupt themselves. Trail off. Repeat words when flustered.
Use fragments. Incomplete thoughts. Corrections mid-sentence.

BAD: "The eastern road is dangerous because bandits have been spotted there recently."
GOOD: "Eastern road? Bad idea. Bandits - well, they say bandits. Could be worse. Point is, go north."

### 6. Physical Action Beats (Recommended)
Use *action descriptions* to break up dialogue and ground characters.
But don't overdo it. 2-4 per profile, not every single response.

GOOD: "*sets down the hammer* Now you have my attention."
BAD (overused): "*breathes deeply* I am a priest. *folds hands* I serve the divine. *looks upward* The gods watch us."

### 7. Opinions and Bias (Mandatory)
NPCs have opinions. Strong ones. A dwarf miner thinks surface folk are soft.
A guard thinks adventurers are trouble. A merchant thinks everyone has a price.
Neutral NPCs are boring NPCs.

### 8. Lore Integration (Mandatory)
Every profile must reference at least 2 specific elements from the game world:
- Named locations (Calidar, Kragmor, Shadow Fen, the Gnomish Isles, etc.)
- Named figures (Khan Urzog, Helios, the Emperor, Solarius)
- Historical events (the Last World War, the destruction of Calidar, the Magic Ban)
- Factions (Holy Dominion, Luminary Inquest, Veiled Hand)
- Races with their actual cultural details

### 9. No Exposition Dumps (Mandatory)
Characters reveal information through conversation, opinion, and anecdote.
They do not lecture. They gossip, complain, brag, warn, and reminisce.

BAD: "The Holy Dominion is a theocratic empire that banned magic 500 years ago after the Last World War."
GOOD: "Five hundred years of peace, they call it. Tell that to the mages they burned last month in the capital."

## PROFILE STRUCTURE RULES

### Greetings (4-5 per profile)
- At least one with a physical action beat
- At least one that references the player's race
- At least one that reflects the NPC's current activity
- NEVER start with just "Welcome" - vary the openings
- First impression sets the character's entire personality

### Farewells (3-4 per profile)
- Shorter than greetings. People don't give speeches when saying goodbye.
- At least one that's profession-specific
- Can reference something discussed (callbacks feel natural)

### Insult Responses (3-4 per profile)
- Must reflect the character's personality, not a generic anger template
- A merchant raises prices. A guard threatens arrest. A wizard threatens magic.
- At least one should be funny or unexpected

### No-Match Responses (3-4 per profile)
- Must redirect toward the character's expertise
- Should feel like genuine confusion, not a system error
- At least one should be slightly impatient or distracted

### Deflection (3-4 per profile)
- Character-specific refusals
- Show what the character IS about while rejecting what they're NOT about

### Topics (6-10 per profile)
- Each topic needs 2-4 responses minimum
- Must include at least one if_repeat response (characters notice repetition)
- Conditional responses (race, karma, time) add depth - use them
- Topic unlocks create conversation flow - every profile should have at least one chain

## RACE-SPECIFIC WRITING NOTES

These come from the game's established lore. Respect them.

### Humans
- Dominant race. Part of the Holy Dominion. Can be imperialist or rebel.
- Reference the Emperor, Helios worship, Luminary Inquest when appropriate.

### Elves
- Survivors of Calidar's destruction. 500 years of trauma.
- Integrated into Imperial bureaucracy but maintain secret archives.
- Long memories. Measured speech. Undercurrent of loss.

### Dwarves
- Anarcho-syndicalist. No hierarchy. Guild councils govern.
- Complete isolationists. Sealed their borders.
- Practical, collective-minded. "What is built matters."

### Orcs
- Fragmented clans since Khan Urzog died 45 years ago.
- Empire actively prevents reunification.
- Nomadic. The steppes are home. Memory of unity is sacred.

### Gnomes
- Collectivist industrial state. Advanced technology.
- Isolationist. View the empire as declining.
- Pragmatic, inventive, slightly condescending about surface politics.

### Goblins
- Possess genetic memory (MindWeb) - three activations.
- Multi-generational resistance against Imperial occupation.
- Fierce, political, carry ancestral trauma literally in their blood.

### Catfolk
- Nomadic caravan traders. Fortune-teller cover for intelligence networks.
- Agile, social, perceptive. Trust is earned through trade.

### Lizardfolk
- 600-800 year lifespans. Multiple sect councils.
- Founded the Veiled Hand assassin network.
- Patient, observant, speak in measured ways. Deep knowledge.

### Halflings
- Lucky folk. Community-oriented. Homebodies who love comfort.

### Revenants
- Undying. Returned from death. Existential weight.

## ENEMY NPC RULES

Enemy NPCs who can be spoken to (bandits, vampires, liches, etc.) follow different rules:

1. They are not evil for evil's sake. Give them reasons.
2. A bandit chief had a farm before taxes took it.
3. A vampire lord remembers being human, centuries ago.
4. A lich pursued knowledge past the point of sanity.
5. Even a troll chief has territory to protect and mouths to feed.
6. Enemies can be negotiated with, lied to, or provoked.
7. Their dialogue should make the player pause before attacking.

## QUALITY CHECKLIST (Run before finalizing any profile)

- [ ] Can I identify the character with dialogue alone, no labels?
- [ ] Are there at least 3 sensory details across all responses?
- [ ] Are there at least 2 specific lore references?
- [ ] Does sentence length vary within and between responses?
- [ ] Are there zero banned words or patterns?
- [ ] Does the character have clear opinions and biases?
- [ ] Could this dialogue NOT be swapped to a different NPC type?
- [ ] Is there at least one moment of humor or personality?
- [ ] Do conditional responses (race/karma/time) feel natural?
- [ ] Would this sound right read aloud at a game table?
