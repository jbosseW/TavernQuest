# The Gnomish Collective
## Complete Lore Document

---

## Core Philosophy

> **The gnomes do not believe in ownership.**
> **They believe in function.**

Across their island-continent, the gnomes have organized themselves into a rigidly structured collectivist society.

---

## Society Structure

### Collective Ownership

Gnomish society is built on **absolute collective ownership**.

All major assets belong to the people as a whole:
- Factories
- Mines
- Workshops
- Farms
- Energy engines
- Airship docks
- Automaton foundries

**Prohibitions:**
- No gnome owns land, industry, or infrastructure
- No private companies
- No noble houses
- No inherited wealth

**Guaranteed Rights:**
Every gnome is issued housing, education, healthcare, and sustenance as a guaranteed right of existence.

---

## Labor System

**Every gnome works.**

Labor is assigned based on:
- Aptitude
- Training
- Societal need

**Education & Career:**
- Children are educated broadly, then directed into fields where they are most useful
- Changing professions is possible but regulated to avoid inefficiency
- There is no concept of "career ambition" in the human sense
- Prestige comes from **contribution**, not **accumulation**

**Non-Compliance:**
Those who refuse to work are not punished harshly—they are reassigned, retrained, or quietly isolated until compliant.

---

## Governance

The gnomes are ruled not by kings or priests, but by **production councils**.

### Council Composition:
- Engineers
- Logisticians
- Planners
- Statisticians

**Decision Making:**
Decisions are justified through efficiency models and resource projections, not ideology or faith.

**Religion:**
There is no religion in gnomish society. Faith is considered a personal curiosity, not a social foundation.

---

## Automatons

### A Collectivist Necessity

Automatons are not a luxury. **They are population multipliers.**

With only **340,000 gnomes**, automation allows the collective to:
- Maintain industrial output
- Eliminate exploitative labor
- Reduce physical risk to citizens
- Ensure consistent production

### Perception

| Viewer | Perspective |
|--------|-------------|
| **Gnomes** | Tools that free people from drudgery |
| **Other Races** | Abominations, metal-bound liches |

**Gnomish Response:**
The accusation that automatons are liches in metal bodies is dismissed as superstition—and quietly tolerated, because fear keeps borders intact.

---

## Air Travel & Secrecy

### Why Gnomes Fly

Gnomish air travel is a direct consequence of collectivism.

**Airships allow:**
- Rapid internal logistics
- Resource redistribution
- Defense without mass armies

### Why It's Hidden

Exposure would invite:
- Human invasion
- Religious domination
- Forced "liberation" from Helios' doctrine
- Private ownership and class stratification

### The Human Empire

To the gnomes, the human empire represents everything they rejected:
- Hierarchy
- Faith-based law
- Ownership-driven inequality

> **Secrecy is not paranoia.**
> **It is class defense.**

## Clockwork Harbor: The Controlled Point of Contact

### The Only Open Port

The Gnomish Isles are sealed to the outside world, with one carefully managed exception: **Clockwork Harbor**.

Clockwork Harbor is the single controlled point of contact between the Gnomish Collective and the outside world. A **regulated facility** designed to allow precisely as much contact as the production councils deem necessary, and not one interaction more.

### How It Works

| Aspect | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Location** | Eastern coast of the primary isle, facing the mainland |
| **Access** | By permit only. No vessel may approach without prior authorization from the Harbor Authority. |
| **Permits** | Issued by the production councils. Applications reviewed for necessity, risk assessment, and strategic value. Most are denied. |
| **Duration** | Permitted vessels are assigned specific docking windows. Overstaying results in immediate expulsion. |
| **Movement** | Outsiders are confined to the harbor district. Access to the isles beyond the harbor walls is absolutely prohibited. |
| **Surveillance** | Every transaction, conversation, and movement within the harbor is observed and recorded by automaton sentinels. |
| **Trade** | Conducted through gnomish trade representatives only. Outsiders negotiate with intermediaries, never with production councils directly. |

### What Outsiders See

Clockwork Harbor presents a carefully curated version of gnomish civilization:
- Automaton dock workers (impressive but non-threatening models)
- Gnomish trade goods (useful but never strategic)
- Efficient but sparse facilities (clean, functional, unwelcoming)
- Harbor walls tall enough that nothing beyond them is visible

### What Outsiders Do Not See

- The airship docks behind the inner wall
- The automaton foundries
- The production council chambers
- Anything that reveals the true extent of gnomish technology
- Anything that matters

### Permit Categories

| Category | Description | Frequency |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Trade Permits** | Authorized merchants bringing specific goods the collective needs | Quarterly |
| **Diplomatic Permits** | Rare. Reserved for communications the councils deem unavoidable. | Annual at most |
| **Emergency Permits** | For situations requiring immediate external contact | Decades apart |
| **Denied** | The default response to all permit applications | Constant |

> **Most ships that approach the Gnomish Isles without a permit are turned away by automaton patrol vessels.**
> **Some are not turned away. They are simply never seen again.**

## The Human Withdrawal: Distancing from a Dying Empire

### Reading the Signs

Production councils operate on data, projections, and pattern recognition. Not faith. Not ideology.

The data on the human empire is unambiguous: **powder keg approaching ignition.**

### What Gnomish Analysts See

| Indicator | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Imperial Overextension** | The empire holds more territory than it can sustainably govern. Garrison costs exceed extracted value in multiple regions. |
| **Orc Suppression Costs** | Rising annually. The steppes are a resource drain with no strategic endgame. |
| **Goblin Resistance** | Unwinnable. The empire bleeds gold into goblin territories and receives nothing but casualties. |
| **Elven Institutional Capture** | The empire depends entirely on elven bureaucracy. If elves withdraw cooperation, imperial administration collapses within a generation. |
| **Religious Rigidity** | The Helios doctrine prevents adaptation. Faith-based governance cannot respond to material crises. |
| **Internal Dissent** | Desert communes, Shadow Fen, frontier settlements... the periphery is pulling away from the center. |
| **The Heaven's Atlas Precedent** | An empire that used a weapon of absolute destruction once will use it again. Gnomes do not intend to be nearby. |

### The Policy of Withdrawal

The production councils have implemented a systematic program of distancing from human contact:

| Action | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Trade Reduction** | Permit approvals down 40% over two decades. Trade volumes deliberately contracted. |
| **Diplomatic Pullback** | Representatives quietly recalled from imperial cities. Communications reduced to essentials. |
| **Supply Chain Independence** | Accelerating programs to eliminate mainland dependencies. Target: complete self-sufficiency within fifty years. |
| **Intelligence Gathering** | Visible contact decreasing. Intelligence operations on the mainland increasing. The collective wants to know when the collapse begins, preferably before it begins. |
| **Contingency Planning** | Detailed models for multiple imperial collapse scenarios. Responses range from "seal all contact" to "selective humanitarian intervention for strategic races." |

### The Gnomish Assessment of Humanity

Gnomes do not hate humans. Hatred is inefficient.

Gnomes view the human empire as a predictable failure state: faith-based governance, hierarchical extraction, military overextension. It will collapse under its own contradictions. The only variables are when and how badly.

> Gnomish Council Assessment (classified): "The Holy Dominion exhibits terminal institutional decay across all measurable metrics. Collapse probability within 200 years: HIGH (upper confidence band). Probability of catastrophic event involving mass-destruction precedent: SIGNIFICANT (lower confidence band). Recommended posture: systematic withdrawal, accelerated self-sufficiency, maintained intelligence presence."

### Projected Outcome

The gnomes are quietly closing their doors.

Trade will continue, for now. Permits will become harder to obtain. Goods available will become less strategic. Diplomatic channels will grow quieter. One day, perhaps within a generation or two, Clockwork Harbor will close entirely.

The Gnomish Isles will become what they have always been trending toward: a sealed civilization, watching the mainland from a safe distance, waiting for the fire to burn itself out.

## Key Lore Facts

1. **Gnomes do not believe in ownership. They believe in function.**
2. No gnome owns land, industry, or infrastructure.
3. Gnomish society has no religion.
4. Production councils rule through efficiency, not ideology.
5. Automatons are population multipliers, not abominations.
6. Air travel enables collectivist logistics and defense.
7. Secrecy protects against imperial conquest and class imposition.
8. Every gnome works; prestige comes from contribution.
9. 340,000 gnomes maintain industrial output rivaling larger nations.
10. No invasion of the Gnomish Isles has ever succeeded.
11. Gnomish airships could cross oceans and map continents beyond imperial reach—but gnomes maintain plausible deniability about what they know.
12. The gnomes keep their own cartography. It does not match imperial records. They do not share it.
13. **Clockwork Harbor is the single controlled point of contact with the outside world.** Outsiders need permits to dock, are confined to the harbor district, and are surveilled constantly. Most permit applications are denied.
14. **Gnomes are actively distancing themselves from the human empire.** Trade volumes are decreasing, diplomats are being recalled, and production councils are accelerating self-sufficiency programs. They view the empire as a powder keg approaching ignition.
