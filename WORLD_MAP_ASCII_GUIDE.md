## DESIGN NOTES

### **Why 280km?**

**Historical Naval Invasion Distances:**
- D-Day (England → France): 34km ✓ Succeeded
- Spanish Armada (Spain → England): 1,600km via Atlantic ✗ Failed
- Japanese invasion attempts of Korea: 200km ✓ Succeeded (13th century)
- Mongol invasion of Japan: 200km ✗ Failed (twice, typhoons)

**Sweet Spot**: 200-300km
- Long enough to make invasion extremely costly
- Short enough that trade is still viable
- Weather becomes major factor
- Multi-day exposure to defenders

**280km is perfect because:**
- Too far for casual crossing (eliminates raids)
- Long enough for storms to matter (nature as ally)
- Extended exposure lets defenders prepare (airships spot fleet days out)
- Supply requirements become prohibitive (must carry week+ of provisions)
- Retreat becomes impossible mid-journey (point of no return)
- But trade ships can still make crossing regularly (commerce continues)

### **Gameplay Impact**

**For Players:**
- Gnomish Isles feel genuinely isolated and mysterious
- Ocean crossing is significant quest/journey (week-long expedition)
- Reinforces gnomish independence (players understand why empire can't invade)
- Creates natural "end-game" content (reaching the isles is achievement)

**For Lore:**
- Justifies 500 years of gnomish independence
- Explains why gnomes remain secretive (defensible position)
- Makes airship technology strategically decisive (naval power obsolete)
- Supports "no invasion has ever succeeded" claim (now believable)
