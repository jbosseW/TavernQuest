# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**The Known World** (working title: Tavern Times RPG) is a dark fantasy open-world RPG set 500 years after an apocalyptic war that nearly destroyed civilization. Players explore a morally complex world where an authoritarian empire maintains order through surveillance and documentation, while independent powers resist from the margins and survivors of genocide navigate their integration into the system that destroyed them.

## Quick Facts

- **Setting**: Dark fantasy world, Imperial Year 500 (500 years post-war)
- **Genre**: Open-world RPG with business simulation, crafting, and exploration
- **Theme**: Memory, control, survival, and the price of both compliance and resistance
- **World Size**: 64×64 tile main continent + infinite procedural regions + isolated island nations
- **Playable Races**: 8 distinct races with unique cultures and histories
- **Major Factions**: 9 factions with complex relationships and ideologies
- **Game Systems**: 20+ integrated systems (combat, crafting, property, karma, stealth, vampire, etc.)

## Core Pillars

1. **Rich Lore**: 500-year timeline with genocide, integration, resistance, and living witnesses
2. **Moral Complexity**: Every faction has justifications and costs—no simple heroes or villains
3. **Deep Systems**: Property ownership, business management, karma/crime, faction reputation
4. **Open World**: Procedurally generated infinite world with hand-crafted regions
5. **Player Freedom**: Multiple paths (combat, stealth, business, crime, exploration)

# PART I: WORLD LORE

## 1. THE WORLD OVERVIEW

### 1.1 The Age After War

Five hundred years ago, the Last World War nearly erased civilization. Multiple factions wielded magic as a weapon of mass destruction, reshaping borders and annihilating populations. The war did not end through victory—it collapsed under exhaustion, famine, plague, and fear.

The **Holy Dominion** rose from the ashes, deploying a forbidden artifact called **Heaven's Atlas** to destroy the elven homeland of **Calidar**. In a single cataclysmic event, thriving forests vitrified into glass, rivers boiled away, and millions died. Elven sovereignty ended in a generation.

What followed was not peace, but **order**—an order built on control, regulation, and the suppression of anything that might threaten stability again.

### 1.2 The Magic Ban

After witnessing Heaven's Atlas turn Calidar to glass, the victorious powers declared:

> **"Magic is not a right. It is a weapon."**

Magic became illegal unless:
- **State-sanctioned**: Licensed by imperial authority, constantly monitored
- **Divinely ordained**: Blessed by Helios the Sun God through his priests

**Unsanctioned magic** became treason, punishable by **execution and ritual soul destruction** (complete erasure from existence).

The empire justifies this by pointing to the Wastes of Calidar: *"This is what happens when power goes unchecked. Would you risk this again?"*

### 1.3 Current State of the World

**Dominant Power**: The Holy Dominion controls the central continent with 1.5 million people (humans + integrated elves).

**Independent Powers**:
- **Dwarven Holds**: Anarcho-syndicalist underground cities (northern mountains)
- **Gnomish Collective**: Techno-communist island nation (280km across the ocean)
- **Orc Clans**: Fragmented nomadic superpower (western steppes)

**Resistance/Underground**:
- **Shadow Fen Commune**: Magically concealed refuge protected by devil pacts
- **Goblin Resistance**: Decentralized guerrilla cells waging multi-generational insurgency
- **The Veiled Hand**: Secret assassin organization preventing escalation to mass atrocity

**Marginalized**:
- **Elves**: Genocide survivors now serving as imperial bureaucracy
- **Beast Folk**: Stateless diaspora heavily scrutinized by authorities
- **Lizard Folk**: Ancient hidden civilization beneath desert sands

## 2. GEOGRAPHY & REGIONS

### 2.1 The Main Continent

**Size**: 64×64 tiles (320km × 320km = 100,000 sq km = 38,690 sq miles)

#### 2.1.1 The Holy Dominion (Central)
- **Territory**: X[25-55], Y[25-50] (31×26 tiles)
- **Population**: ~1,500,000 (1M humans + 500K elves)
- **Capital**: Imperial Capital with Grand Cathedral of Helios
- **Control**: Political and religious center of the known world
- **Enforcement**: The Luminary Inquest (white-cloaked enforcers)
- **Status**: Dominant continental power

**Key Features**:
- Most developed and populated region
- Integrated elven districts in southern areas
- Heavy Luminary Inquest presence
- Extensive bureaucracy run by elves
- Documentation required for all movement and identity

#### 2.1.2 Dwarven Mountains (North)
- **Territory**: X[18-45], Y[0-18] (28×19 tiles)
- **Population**: ~185,000 (all underground)
- **Capital**: The Deep Holds (interconnected underground cities)
- **Government**: Guild councils with rotating leadership
- **Philosophy**: "Labor belongs to those who give it. Stone belongs to those who work it."
- **Status**: Independent, isolationist, closed borders

**Key Features**:
- Anarcho-syndicalist society with no hierarchy
- Stone-born reproduction (no biological parents)
- Collective ownership of all productive spaces
- Trade through fortified surface gates only
- Never been successfully invaded

#### 2.1.3 Orcish Steppes (West)
- **Territory**: X[8-28], Y[15-35] (21×21 tiles)
- **Population**: ~240,000 (mobile, following seasonal routes)
- **Settlement**: Kragmor (semi-permanent trading hub)
- **Government**: Fragmented clans (no current Khan)
- **Legacy**: Once the most effective military force ever known
- **Status**: Dormant superpower—watched nervously by empire

**Key Features**:
- Nomadic steppe civilization
- Preserve laws, routes, and commands for reunification
- Imperial fear: "They only need to unite again"
- Sophisticated mobile warfare doctrine
- Living elders remember unified campaigns

#### 2.1.4 Shadowfen (Southwest)
- **Territory**: X[5-25], Y[45-63] (21×19 tiles)
- **Population**: 8,000-12,000 (empire believes "uninhabited")
- **Government**: Secret council (membership unknown)
- **Protection**: The Veil (magical concealment field)
- **Allies**: Infernal entities (devils and demons)
- **Status**: Hidden commune, imperial containment zone

**Key Features**:
- Magically concealed swamplands
- Founded by refugees fleeing imperial persecution
- Infernal pacts harden borders
- Communal labor, burned identities, past lives don't matter
- Houses The Veiled Hand assassin organization
- Luminary Inquest classifies as "corrupted exclusion zone" and avoids it

#### 2.1.5 Wastes of Calidar (South)
- **Territory**: Y ≥ 64 (extends infinitely south)
- **Population**: 0 (lifeless)
- **Former Name**: Calidar (elven forest homeland)
- **Destruction**: 500 years ago via Heaven's Atlas
- **Current State**: Endless glass desert, vitrified forests
- **Status**: Forbidden zone, monument to war

**Key Features**:
- Former elven civilization (thousands of years old)
- Destroyed in single cataclysmic event
- Forests → glass, rivers → vapor, stone → sand
- 80-90% of elven population killed (millions)
- Serves as imperial justification for magic ban
- Return forbidden by imperial decree
- Strange lights and whispers reported by travelers

### 2.2 Desert Regions

#### 2.2.1 Great Endless Desert (North)
- **Territory**: Y < 0 (infinite north)
- **Terrain**: 97% empty, sparse encounters
- **Population**: Scattered beast folk, hidden lizard folk
- **Features**: Origin homeland of beast folk diaspora
- **Secret**: Lizard folk hidden river civilization beneath sands

#### 2.2.2 Scorched Sands (West)
- **Territory**: X < 0 (infinite west)
- **Terrain**: Desert wasteland, natural barrier
- **Population**: Minimal
- **Function**: Separates settled lands from unknown west

### 2.3 Ocean & Islands

#### 2.3.1 The Shimmering Sea (Silver Seas)
- **Territory**: X[64-119] (56 tiles / 280 kilometers wide)
- **Type**: Open ocean between continent and Gnomish Isles
- **Crossing Time**: 5-7 days by sailing ship
- **Hazards**: Storms, sea creatures (serpents), extended exposure
- **Strategic**: Prevents naval invasion of Gnomish Isles

**Why No Invasion Has Succeeded**:
- 280km of open water (no islands, no stopping points)
- Week-long exposure to weather and gnomish defenses
- Gnomish airships spot approaching fleets days in advance
- Unknown gnomish coastal weapons (automatons, aerial bombardment)
- Extended supply requirements make invasion prohibitively expensive
- Historical record: ZERO successful invasions in 500 years

#### 2.3.2 Gnomish Isles (East)
- **Territory**: X[120-149], Y[25-54] (30×30 tiles)
- **Population**: ~340,000 (14.6% of world)
- **Cities**: Mechspire (industrial capital), Clockwork Harbor (trade port)
- **Government**: Production councils (engineers, logisticians, planners)
- **Philosophy**: "Gnomes do not believe in ownership. They believe in function."
- **Status**: Independent collectivist industrial state

**Key Features**:
- Absolute collective ownership (no private property)
- Advanced automatons as population multipliers
- Secret airship fleets (complete air superiority)
- No religion (faith is personal curiosity)
- Total isolation to prevent imperial invasion
- Interior cities closed to outsiders (trade at ports only)
- "Secrecy is class defense"

## 3. THE LAST WORLD WAR & HEAVEN'S ATLAS

### 3.1 The War That Nearly Ended Everything

**Timeline**: 500 years ago (Year -500 to Year 1)

The apocalyptic conflict that reshaped the world. Multiple factions wielded magic as weapons of mass destruction. Borders were redrawn through annihilation. Populations were erased. Reality itself bent under competing spells.

Cities burned. Armies vanished. The war did not end through victory—it collapsed under exhaustion, famine, plague, and fear.

### 3.2 Heaven's Atlas - The Weapon That Changed Everything

**Type**: Magical artifact of apocalyptic power
**Deployed By**: Forces that became the Holy Dominion
**Target**: The elven homeland of Calidar
**Effect**: Forests vitrified, rivers boiled, stone melted to glass
**Casualties**: Millions of elves killed instantly (80-90% of population)
**Outcome**: War ended shortly after—no faction wanted to be next

**Current Location**: Unknown
- **Official claim**: Destroyed after the war to prevent misuse
- **Speculation**: Hidden in Grand Cathedral's deepest vaults OR shattered into pieces and scattered
- **Significance**: Justification for eternal magic ban. Proof that magic can erase civilization.

### 3.3 Consequences of Heaven's Atlas

**Immediate** (Year 1):
1. Elven sovereignty ended in a single generation
2. Magic banned except state-sanctioned or divinely ordained
3. Unsanctioned magic became capital crime (execution + soul destruction)
4. Surviving elves integrated into Holy Empire (survival, not choice)
5. Refugees founded Shadow Fen in southwestern swamps
6. Lizard folk sect created The Veiled Hand after witnessing destruction

**Long-Term** (500 years):
1. Wastes of Calidar serve as permanent monument and warning
2. Empire points to it whenever justifying control
3. Elves remember everything and document imperial abuses
4. The Veiled Hand eliminates officials who would create another Calidar
5. Every elf knows the names of lost cities
6. **The Silent Question**: "If we could recover Heaven's Atlas, what would we do?"

## 4. MAJOR FACTIONS

### 4.1 The Holy Dominion

**Type**: Theocratic imperial state
**Population**: 1,500,000 (humans + integrated elves)
**Territory**: Central continent
**Leader**: The Emperor (divine mandate of Helios)
**Deity**: Helios, the Sun God
**Doctrine**: "Magic is not a right. It is a weapon."

**Sub-Organization: The Luminary Inquest**
- **Common Names**: The Inquest, Luminaries, Sun Enforcers
- **Uniform**: White cloaks with sun insignias
- **Authority**: Supersedes all local law, city guards, regional governance
- **Mandate**: Regulation of existence itself
- **Methods**: Documentation checks, public executions, disappearances
- **Targets**: Vampires, demons, unsanctioned mages, marginalized populations

**How They Enforce**:
1. Random documentation checks (travel papers, residency permits, lineage records)
2. Public accusations and immediate arrests
3. Suspects isolated, restrained, judged on spot
4. Executions conducted openly (visibility reinforces order)
5. Disappearances explained as "administrative resolution"
6. **Danger of innocence**: Missing papers = intent to deceive = detention

**Public Perception**:
- Widely feared and resented
- Taverns fall silent when white cloaks appear
- Markets empty at sight of sun insignias
- Most tolerate brutality as price of safety
- **"Innocence does not guarantee safety"**

**Relations**:
- Elven Administration: Integrated (necessary but resented)
- Dwarven Holds: Neutral trade only
- Orc Clans: Nervous observation (fear of reunification)
- Gnomish Collective: Limited trade (deny entry to isles)
- Shadow Fen: Tolerated as containment zone
- Goblin Resistance: Active suppression ("pest control")

### 4.2 The Elven Administration (The Administered Remnant)

**Type**: Integrated bureaucratic caste
**Population**: 500,000 (21.5% of world)
**Former Homeland**: Calidar (destroyed 500 years ago)
**Current Territory**: Southern Holy Dominion
**Lifespan**: 10,000 years
**Philosophy**: "What is written endures. What is remembered survives."

**The Tragedy of Calidar**:
- Thriving forest realm for thousands of years
- Canopy cities, river archives, magical scholarship
- Destroyed by Heaven's Atlas in single event
- Survivors fled north under imperial escort
- Integration was survival after total military defeat

**Role in Empire**:
- Administrators, archivists, legal scholars, trade regulators
- Manage imperial census, tax collection, court systems
- Run historical archives and documentation
- **"The empire's right hand"**—necessary but never fully trusted

**The Hidden Truth**:
- **"Elves did not destroy magic. They hid it."**
- Sealed archives contain pre-war magical knowledge
- Some quietly aid persecuted mages and fugitives
- Document imperial abuses for historical judgment
- Old Ones (500+ years old) personally witnessed Calidar
- Prepare records for the next empire—when Dominion falls, elves will still be here

**Cultural Preservation** (Illegal but Practiced):
- Teach children Forest Tongue (Calidar dialect)
- Plant Calidar-native trees from saved seeds
- Observe Day of Glass (anniversary of destruction)
- Maintain separate BA/AA calendar (Before Atlas / After Atlas)
- Remember every lost city's name

**The Silent Question**:
Every elf knows but never says aloud: *"If we could recover Heaven's Atlas, what would we do with it?"*
- Destroy it? (Prevent another genocide)
- Hide it? (Keep from imperial hands)
- Use it? (Turn imperial capital to glass)

Empire watches. Elves document the watching. Neither answers the question.

### 4.3 The Free Holds of Stone (Dwarves)

**Type**: Collectivist labor federation
**Population**: 185,000 (7.9% of world)
**Territory**: Northern mountains (underground)
**Lifespan**: 300-500 years
**Government**: Guild councils with rotating leadership
**Philosophy**: "Dwarves do not believe in hierarchy. They believe in labor."

**Society Structure**:
- **Stone-born reproduction**: No biological parents, emerge from sacred chambers
- **Collective ownership**: No private property, all productive spaces belong to the hold
- **Guild governance**: Miners, Smiths, Engineers, Brewers, Stonecutters, Wardens
- **Rotating leadership**: Positions rotate by schedule, not election
- **Equal distribution**: "Every dwarf who works the stone shares its bounty"

**Why It Works**:
- No biological parents = no family-based power structures
- Stone-born = no inheritance disputes
- Long lifespans (200-400 years) = direct institutional memory across centuries
- Scarcity reinforces collectivism (each dwarf represents generations of labor)

**Relations with Surface**:
- **Trade**: Limited and transactional (metals for grain)
- **Politics**: "Surface can fight over crowns. Dwarves build."
- **Imperial attempts**: Refused hierarchy imposition, closed borders
- **Status**: Never conquered, maintains complete independence

**Religion**: None. "Stone is truth, time is law, labor is meaning."

### 4.4 The Orc Clans

**Type**: Nomadic military confederation (fragmented)
**Population**: 240,000 (10.3% of world)
**Territory**: Western steppes
**Lifespan**: 300-500 years
**Government**: None (no current Khan)
**Legacy**: "At their height, the orcish clans formed the most effective military civilization the world has ever known."

**Historical Peak**:
Under a Great Khan (died 60+ years ago), unified clans:
- Reshaped borders and shattered kingdoms
- Conducted continent-spanning campaigns at unprecedented speed
- Sustained armies without fixed supply lines
- Absorbed conquered peoples rather than annihilating them
- Changed warfare forever through psychological collapse tactics

**Current State**:
- Fragmented into independent clans
- Living elders (60-70) remember unification
- Laws, routes, and commands still taught through oral tradition
- View fragmentation as **dormancy, not defeat**
- **"They do not need to grow stronger. They only need to unite again."**

**Warfare Doctrine** (Still Taught):
- Speed over attrition
- Psychological collapse over brute force
- Decentralized command with absolute obedience
- Feigned retreats and encirclement
- Integration of conquered peoples (Roman-style)

**Imperial Response**:
- Propaganda exaggerates orcish savagery
- Justifies constant military readiness
- Generals study orcish campaigns obsessively
- Fear of reunification drives border security
- Standing orders to assassinate potential Khans

---

### 4.5 The Gnomish Collective

**Type**: Collectivist industrial state
**Population**: 340,000 (14.6% of world)
**Territory**: Eastern islands (280km across ocean from mainland)
**Lifespan**: 200-350 years
**Government**: Production councils
**Philosophy**: "Gnomes do not believe in ownership. They believe in function."

**Society Structure**:
- **Absolute collective ownership**: No private property, companies, or inheritance
- **Production councils**: Engineers, logisticians, planners govern through efficiency
- **Every gnome works**: Assigned by aptitude, training, societal need
- **Guaranteed rights**: Housing, education, healthcare, sustenance
- **No religion**: Faith is personal curiosity, not social foundation

**Technology** (Secret from outsiders):
- **Automatons**: Population multipliers freeing gnomes from drudgery
- **Airship fleets**: Rapid logistics, air superiority over any naval invasion
- **Precision industry**: Manufacturing capacity rivaling larger nations
- **Post-scarcity**: 340,000 gnomes maintain industrial output through automation

**Why Secrecy Matters**:
- Exposure would invite imperial invasion
- Religious domination (forced conversion to Helios worship)
- Forced "liberation" from collectivism
- Private ownership and class stratification imposed
- **"Secrecy is not paranoia. It is class defense."**

**Ocean Barrier Defense**:
- 280 kilometers of open water
- 5-7 day naval crossing exposes fleets to storms, sea creatures
- Gnomish airships spot fleets days in advance
- Unknown coastal weapons await
- No successful invasion in 500 years

### 4.6 The Shadow Fen Commune

**Type**: Magically concealed anarchist commune
**Population**: 8,000-12,000 (empire estimates "hundreds")
**Territory**: Southwestern swamplands
**Lifespan**: Mixed (humans, elves, mages, beast folk, orcs, goblins)
**Government**: Secret council (membership unknown)
**Protection**: The Veil (magical concealment) + infernal pacts

**Origins**:
Founded in decades after Last World War by refugees:
- Calidar survivors who refused integration
- Displaced villagers erased by imperial consolidation
- Laborers whose papers failed Luminary Inquest
- Fugitive mages fleeing execution
- Families fleeing after Inquest visits

**The Veil** (Magical Concealment):
- Pervasive field woven into water, fog, root
- Redirects perception of hostile intruders
- Paths loop endlessly, settlements vanish
- Reveals fen only to those fleeing imperial control
- Self-sustaining through resident mages + infernal reinforcement

**Infernal Pacts**:
- Formed with devils and lesser demons after imperial patrols attempted entry
- **Commune offers**: Regular offerings (blood, materials, condemned souls), access to mortal world, knowledge sharing
- **Infernals provide**: Hardened borders, devouring mists, path collapse, enhanced concealment, warning systems
- **Cost**: Some residents bear infernal marks, children born with demonic traits

**Life in the Fen**:
- Communal labor (scarcity allows no other choice)
- Identities burned upon entry (papers destroyed, past lives irrelevant)
- Magic practiced openly but cautiously
- No formal law—unwritten survival code
- **"The swamp doesn't care what you were. Only what you can do."**

**Imperial Tolerance**:
- Classified as "corrupted exclusion zone"
- Functions as containment zone for malcontents
- Absorbs fugitives who might otherwise rebel in cities
- Periodic purges kill 10-30 people every 6-12 months
- Full eradication would cost more than it's worth

### 4.7 The Goblin Resistance

**Type**: Decentralized guerrilla network
**Population**: Unknown (empire doesn't count "vermin")
**Territory**: Scattered cells (mines, sewers, tunnels, ruins)
**Lifespan**: 30-60 years
**Organization**: Autonomous cells of 5-20 goblins
**Philosophy**: "What was taken will be remembered. What is remembered cannot be erased."

**Historical Context**:
- Once held territories across the continent
- Systematically driven out during imperial expansion
- Adapted to existence in margins and forgotten places
- Every goblin knows names of lost homelands
- Teach children through oral tradition

**Organization**:
- **Cell-based structure**: No central command
- **Coordination**: Coded signals, dead drops, oral tradition
- **Resilience**: "Capture one cell, learn nothing about others"
- **Self-sustaining**: "Every warren cleared spawns two more"

**Warfare Doctrine**:
- **"A goblin does not fight wars. A goblin wages resistance."**
- Strike supply lines, not armies
- Sabotage infrastructure (roads, bridges, storage)
- Ambush patrols and isolated units
- Vanish before retaliation arrives
- Make occupation economically unsustainable

**Imperial Response**:
- **Official**: "Pest control" (civic duty to exterminate)
- **Reality**: Raids cost more to prevent than they steal
- **Quartermaster view**: "Budget nightmare"
- **Frustration**: No campaign has ever fully eradicated them

**The Principle**:
> "The occupier must guard everything. The resistance must only strike once."

### 4.8 The Lizard Folk Sects

**Type**: Secretive knowledge confederation
**Population**: ~15,000 (0.6% of world, estimates unreliable)
**Territory**: Hidden rivers beneath northern deserts
**Lifespan**: 600-800 years
**Organization**: Multiple sects (no single authority)
**Philosophy**: "Knowledge over territory. What is hidden endures. What is revealed can be taken."

**The Hidden River Empire**:
- Ancient civilization predating surface empires
- Cities along rivers outsiders rarely found
- Withdrew inward over generations
- Much still exists, hidden beneath desert sands
- Lizard folk won't reveal how much remains

**Sect Domains** (Compartmentalized Knowledge):
1. **River Engineering**: Control of hidden waterways, irrigation
2. **Burial Rites**: Preservation of dead, ancestral memory
3. **Trade Routes**: Knowledge of desert paths, safe passages
4. **Astronomy**: Celestial navigation, calendar keeping
5. **Martial Discipline**: Protection of sect interests, hidden places

**Temporal Power**:
- 600-800 year lifespans = personal memory spanning centuries
- Individual lizard folk alive today remember events from before Last World War
- See patterns humans miss (humans see decades, lizard folk see millennia)
- Founded The Veiled Hand 500 years ago—still led by founders

**Surface Presence**:
When lizard folk appear (as guides, traders, engineers):
- Always purposeful, never random
- True sect affiliations hidden
- Information shared deliberately and incompletely
- Rarely settle outside ancestral regions

### 4.9 The Veiled Hand (The Assassins of Memory)

**Type**: Secretive assassination network
**Population**: 200-500 operatives (estimated)
**Base**: Shadow Fen Commune
**Founded**: Year 1 (500 years ago)
**Founders**: Lizard folk astronomical observation sect
**Philosophy**: "Preventing a single decision can save countless lives."

**Origin Story**:
Lizard folk sect witnessed Heaven's Atlas activation from afar:
- Rivers vanished from their charts
- Star alignments shifted from magical devastation
- Magical currents collapsed into silence
- An entire civilization ended in a single recorded event

**Their Conclusion**:
> "Power had escaped all restraint. Empires armed with absolute force can only be constrained through precision, patience, and removal of key individuals before catastrophe becomes inevitable."

**Mission**:
Ensure Heaven's Atlas is never used again—or that those who would use it do not live long enough to authorize it.

**Targeting Criteria** (Who Gets Killed):
✓ Imperial officials authorizing mass purges
✓ Inquest commanders expanding beyond containment
✓ Military officers planning invasions of independent territories
✓ Those developing weapons of mass magical destruction
✓ Betrayers who would guide patrols into Shadow Fen

**Not Targeted** (Moral Restraint):
✗ Low-level enforcers (following orders, not authorizing)
✗ Corrupt officials (petty evil ≠ existential threat)
✗ Political rivals (not a contract-killing service)
✗ Random citizens (exists to prevent mass death, not cause it)

**Methods**:
- **Preferred**: Staged accidents (falls, drowning, fire)
- **Acceptable**: Poison (slow-acting, appears as natural illness)
- **Rare**: Direct assassination (only when other methods impossible)
- **Never**: Public spectacle (defeats the purpose)

**Organizational Structure**:
- **Compartmentalized cells**: No member knows full scope
- **Initiates**: Highest level (lizard folk founders, 600-800 years old)
- **Operatives**: Trained assassins embedded in society
- **Informants**: Gather intelligence, not authorized to kill
- **Support Network**: Provide shelter, documents, resources

**Deterrence Model**:
Many observed targets are **never struck**—knowing they're watched often changes behavior. Prevention through uncertainty is preferable to violence.

**Imperial Response**:
- **Official**: Denies existence ("paranoid fantasy")
- **Internal**: Patterns too consistent to ignore
- **Reality**: Cannot prove, cannot stop, absorbs losses quietly
- **Stalemate**: Hand removes hardliners at rate empire can absorb

**Moral Question**:
Is extra-judicial killing justified to prevent mass atrocity? The Hand's position: *"We are what the absence of justice created."*

---

### 4.10 Beast Folk (The Cat Folk Diaspora)

**Type**: Stateless diaspora culture
**Population**: 45,200 (1.9% of world; ~30,000 cat folk)
**Territory**: None (scattered across all lands)
**Lifespan**: 50-80 years
**Organization**: Extended family groups
**Philosophy**: "Beast folk do not recognize borders as permanent truths."

**Historical Displacement**:
- Origin: Great deserts beyond known lands
- Displaced by war, drought, expanding empires
- No single kingdom or unified homeland
- Learned to exist within other peoples' lands without belonging

**Survival Strategy**:
- Value portability over possession
- Skills passed orally (can't be confiscated)
- Wealth measured in relationships, not property
- Parallel legal systems (internal mediation when imperial courts fail)
- Move along established family routes

**Cat Folk Specialization**:
Famous for gambling, fortune-telling, performance—**not because of magic**, but because:
- Gambling halls offer success without land, titles, or citizenship
- Pattern recognition refined over generations (statistically literate)
- **"Cat folk do not believe chance is random. They believe it reflects attention, timing, and respect for risk."**

**Treatment by Others**:
> **"Tolerated, distrusted, romanticized, and scapegoated—often at the same time."**

| Economic Condition | Beast Folk Are |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Economy tightening | Blamed first |
| Entertainment desired | Welcomed briefly |

**Luminary Inquest Scrutiny**:
- **Heaviest scrutiny** of any group
- Papers checked relentlessly
- "Administrative discrepancies" used as pretext for detention
- Some elves quietly help with documentation (remember persecution)

**Cultural Transmission** (50-80 year lifespans require efficiency):
- Oral tradition, song, ritual
- Family loyalty paramount
- Betrayal remembered across generations (protective, not vengeful)
- **Goal**: "Ability to move, trade, live, and raise children without persecution"

**Transformation Methods**:
1. Bitten by vampire enemy (15% chance in combat)
2. Transform after being bitten (delayed transformation)
3. Manual transformation (debug/admin)

**Vampire Mechanics**:
- **Stat Doubling**: All combat stats × 2, all D&D stats × 2
- **Sunlight Damage**: Takes damage during day (hours 6-18)
  - Dawn: 5 damage/tick
  - Morning: 15 damage/tick
  - Noon: 30 damage/tick
  - Afternoon: 15 damage/tick
  - Dusk: 5 damage/tick
- **Protection**: Buildings, dungeons, caves, coffins, cloth wrapping
- **Coffin**: Complete sunlight immunity
- **Cloth Wrapping**: 70% protection (30% fail chance per tick)

**Vampire Skill Tree** (1 point per 2 vampire levels):

**Tier 1** (1 point each):
- Blood Drain: Heal 25% of damage dealt
- Night Vision: +20% damage at night
- Mist Form: 50% damage reduction for 3 turns

**Tier 2** (2 points each):
- Bat Swarm: AOE attack on all enemies
- Hypnotic Gaze: 50% less detection when biting NPCs
- Enhanced Regeneration: 5% HP regen per turn

**Tier 3** (3 points each):
- Shadow Step: Teleport/escape combat
- Vampiric Lord: +50% all stats, mist immunity
- Blood Plague: 100% infection rate, 0% detection when biting

**Bite-to-Transform NPCs**:
- Must be vampire, NPC must be asleep
- Base detection: 20% (Hypnotic Gaze: 10%, Blood Plague: 0%)
- If detected: +1000 bounty, Holy City sends vampire hunters
- If successful: NPC becomes vampire, spreads to others (epidemic)

**Vampire Hunters** (Holy City Response):
- Activate when 5+ vampires in a city
- Kill 50% of vampire NPCs during purge
- Trigger when player detected biting NPCs
- Increase Luminary Inquest patrol activity

### 9.4 Stealth & Detection System

**Stealth Mode Toggle**:
- Click slide tab on left panel to toggle on/off
- **Stealth ON**: 🌑 green knob, -25% base detection
- **Stealth OFF**: 🌞 gray knob, normal detection
- Shows current detection chance when toggled

**Detection Formula**:
```
Final Detection = (Base × Location Mod) + Class Mod + Equipment Mod + Stealth Mod + Skill Mod
```

**Time of Day Modifiers**:
- Late Night (22-4): 20% detection (best)
- Dawn (5-6): 50-70%
- Morning/Afternoon (7-17): 90%
- Noon (12-13): 100% (worst)
- Dusk (18): 60%
- Evening (19-21): 40%

**Location Modifiers**:
- Building interior: ×0.3 (-70% detection)
- Shadows/alleys: ×0.5 (-50%)
- Wilderness: ×0.6 (-40%)
- Crowded market: ×0.7 (-30%)
- Open street (night): ×1.0 (normal)
- Town square: ×1.2 (+20%)
- Open street (day): ×1.5 (+50%)

**Class Bonuses**:
- Rogue: -30% detection
- Assassin specialization: -40%
- Vampire (night): -25%
- Warrior: +10% (heavy armor)

**Equipment**:
- Plate armor: +15%
- Leather armor: -5%
- Stealth cloak: -20%
- Dark hood: -10%
- Soft boots: -10%

**Detection Caps**:
- Minimum: 1% (always small chance)
- Maximum: 100% (guaranteed)
- With stealth mode: Capped at 75%

- Aid elves who document but don't rebel?
- Support assassins who kill to prevent escalation?

### 16.2 Living History Through Lifespan Differences

**Unique Mechanic**: Different races remember different amounts of history

- **Humans** (60-90 years): Forget quickly, repeat mistakes, short-term thinking
- **Gnomes** (200-350 years): Institutional memory across centuries
- **Dwarves/Orcs** (300-500 years): Multi-generational cultural memory
- **Lizard Folk** (600-800 years): Personal memory spanning entire civilizations
- **Elves** (10,000 years): Living witnesses across ages, remember empires that existed before human civilization

**Gameplay Impact**:
- Elven NPCs over 500 years old **personally witnessed** Calidar's destruction
- Lizard folk founders of Veiled Hand still alive and active
- Dwarven elders remember imperial attempts to impose hierarchy 300 years ago
- Human officials rotate every 30-50 years while elves remember 700

**Narrative Depth**:
Players can meet NPCs who:
- Saw the glass desert when it was forests
- Remember the empire's founding
- Witnessed every imperial abuse personally
- Are preparing for the empire's fall (because all empires fall)

### 16.3 Genocide Survivors as Bureaucrats

**Unprecedented Fantasy Trope**:
Most fantasy: Survivors rebel or vanish
This game: Survivors **administer the system that destroyed them**

**Elven Tragedy**:
- Homeland turned to glass 500 years ago
- 80-90% of population killed
- Integration was survival after total defeat
- Now process imperial documents, run census, enforce laws
- **Every elf knows who destroyed them and serves them anyway**

**Subtle Resistance**:
- "Clerical errors" that save lives
- Census gaps creating invisibility for fugitives
- Warnings to those about to be investigated
- Sealed archives preserving forbidden knowledge
- **"We comply because we must. We remember because we can."**

**Player Impact**:
- Interact with elves who personally remember Calidar
- See the cost of survival (serving your destroyers)
- Moral questions: Is compliance betrayal? Is survival enough?

### 16.4 Multiple Valid Ideological Systems

**Three Working Collectivist Alternatives to Empire**:

1. **Dwarven Anarcho-Syndicalism**:
   - Guild councils, rotating leadership, no hierarchy
   - Stone-born biology makes family-based power impossible
   - Proves collective ownership can work

2. **Gnomish Techno-Communism**:
   - Production councils, automatons as labor
   - Post-scarcity through technology
   - Proves communism can succeed with automation

3. **Shadow Fen Accidental Commune**:
   - Collectivism from necessity, not ideology
   - Proves scarcity creates mutual aid naturally
   - But protected by devil pacts (moral cost)

**Player Exploration**:
- Visit all three systems
- See different solutions to same problems
- No system is perfect (all have costs)
- No obvious "best" choice

### 16.5 Documentation as Weapon

**Unique Control Mechanic**: The empire controls through **paperwork**, not just military

**The Luminary Inquest's Real Power**:
- Not armies—**documentation requirements**
- Missing papers = intent to deceive = detention
- Beast folk inspected relentlessly
- "Administrative discrepancies" = disappearances
- **Bureaucracy weaponized against marginalized**

**Elven Counter-Weapon**:
- Elves control the archives
- "Lost" documents, delayed requests, convenient errors
- Census gaps create invisibility
- **"They destroyed our homeland. We document theirs. History will judge."**

**Player Impact**:
- Must manage documentation (travel papers, permits)
- Can forge documents (lockpicking + criminal path)
- Can work with elven clerks for "administrative assistance"
- Experience surveillance state mechanics

### 16.6 Temporal Strategy (The Long Game)

**Factions Playing Different Time Scales**:

- **Humans**: 60-90 years (immediate gains, short memory)
- **Gnomes**: 200-350 years (industrial planning across generations)
- **Dwarves/Orcs**: 300-500 years (cultural continuity, institutional knowledge)
- **Lizard Folk**: 600-800 years (multi-empire strategy, founded Veiled Hand 500 years ago)
- **Elves**: 10,000 years (preparing for empires after this empire falls, remember ages before humans existed)
- **Goblins**: 30-60 years but multi-generational mission

**Strategic Depth**:
- Empire thinks in decades
- Elves think in centuries
- Lizard folk think in millennia
- **The patient inherit the earth**

**Player Questions**:
- Who's actually winning?
- Is imperial control temporary?
- Are long-lived races just waiting for empire to collapse?

### 16.7 Memory as Resistance

**Central Theme Mechanized**:

**Goblins**: "What is remembered cannot be erased."
- Teach children names of lost homelands
- Resistance self-sustains through cultural memory
- Empire can't erase them because memory outlasts occupation

**Elves**: "What is written endures."
- Document every imperial abuse
- Sealed archives ensure truth survives
- When empire falls, elves will have complete historical record

**Lizard Folk**: Personal memory spanning centuries
- Witnessed Calidar from afar
- Founded Veiled Hand as response
- 600-800 year lifespans = living libraries

**Player Role**:
- Discover these stories
- Choose which memories to honor
- Decide if compliance or resistance preserves more
