# The Orc Clans
## Complete Lore Document

---

## Historical Legacy

At their height, the orcish clans formed the most effective military civilization the world has ever known.

Under the rule of a Great Khan---whose name is still recorded cautiously in human and elven archives---the orcs:
- Reshaped borders
- Shattered kingdoms
- Permanently changed the nature of war

> Even in the present age, long after that unity fractured, the memory of it governs imperial policy.

---

## Nomadic Society

The orcs live as a nomadic steppe civilization bound not to land, but to movement.

### Structure
- Clans follow seasonal routes across western grasslands, steppes, and badlands
- Permanent cities are rare and intentionally temporary
- Society bound by movement, not settlement

### Training
Every orc is raised to ride, fight, and obey command.

---

## The Era of Unification

During the era of unification, rival clans were bound together under a single Great Khan.

Through merit, fear, and absolute authority, the clans became a single war machine.

### Achievements Under Unification

Under this rule, the orcs achieved what no other race managed:

| Achievement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Rapid Campaigns** | Conducted continent-spanning campaigns at unprecedented speed |
| **Self-Sufficiency** | Sustained armies without fixed supply lines |
| **Coordination** | Clan leaders coordinated across hundreds of kilometers using drum-relay, rider chains, and a signal system the empire spent eighty years trying to decode and never fully did. |
| **Integration** | Absorbed conquered peoples rather than annihilating them |

### Method of Conquest

> The orcs struck with speed, gathered what they needed, and moved on.
> Resistance was punished ruthlessly.
> Submission was rewarded with protection and integration.

---

## Warfare Doctrine

Orcish warfare is built on speed, intelligence, and psychological collapse---not brute force.

### Core Principles

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Mobility** | Constant movement, never static defense |
| **Command** | Decentralized command backed by absolute obedience |
| **Communication** | Sophisticated signaling through riders, banners, and horns |
| **Tactics** | Feigned retreats and encirclement are common |

### Philosophy of War

> They do not fight wars of attrition.
> They fight wars of collapse.

An enemy that loses cohesion is already defeated.

### The Weight of Centuries

Where other races train soldiers for a decade and call them veterans, the orcs produce warriors with lifetimes of combat experience measured in centuries. A senior orc cavalry commander may have personally fought in two hundred campaigns across three hundred years of active service. Arithmetic, not metaphor.

Imperial military analysts understand this disparity. A human general with forty years of experience faces an orc warlord with four hundred. The knowledge gap is not tactical---it is civilizational.

## Law & Order

Orc society is governed by a strict and universal legal code.

### Legal Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Internal Justice** | Theft within the clan is punished severely |
| **Military Discipline** | Disobedience during war is unforgivable |
| **Meritocracy** | Rank is determined by merit, not blood |
| **Loyalty** | Loyalty to the Khan overrides all other bonds |
| **Equality** | The law applies equally to all---including leaders |

### Perception

| Viewer | Perception |
|--------|------------|
| **Outsiders** | Appears brutal |
| **Orcs** | The reason their society survives |

### Legal Continuity Across Centuries

Orcish legal tradition benefits enormously from longevity. A judge who rendered a verdict two hundred years ago may still sit in judgment today. Precedent is not academic---it is personal memory. The orc who wrote the code still rides. The orc who enforced it still draws breath. This gives orcish law a consistency and weight that shorter-lived civilizations cannot replicate.

## Orcish Culture Beyond the Saddle

Outsiders see mounted warriors and assume that is all the orcs are. They are wrong. With lifespans of three to five centuries, orcish culture has developed a depth and sophistication that rivals any settled civilization.

### Art and Craft

Orcish artisanship is shaped by two constraints: nomadic life demands portability, and orcish lifespans allow mastery beyond anything shorter-lived races can achieve.

| Craft | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Leatherwork** | Steppe leather is tooled, dyed, and layered with patterns that encode clan histories. A master leatherworker with two centuries of practice produces pieces that human collectors pay fortunes for---when they can acquire them at all. |
| **Bone Carving** | The bones of great beasts are carved into knife handles, belt clasps, saddle ornaments, and memorial totems. The finest bone carvers can render a full clan genealogy onto a single rib. |
| **Banner-Weaving** | Clan banners are woven from horsehair, dyed with mineral pigments, and embroidered with campaign records. A war banner that has flown over three centuries of battles is considered sacred. It is never retired---only repaired, layered, reinforced. The oldest banners are thick as armor from accumulated stitching. |
| **Metalwork** | Orcish smiths forge blades, arrowheads, and horse tack designed for centuries of use. An orc sword is balanced for its wielder's hand and rebalanced as the wielder ages and their grip changes over decades. |
| **Felt and Textile** | Ger (tent) construction is an art form. The finest gers are weatherproof, portable, and decorated with internal hangings that depict the family's history across generations. |

### Music and Song

Orcish music is the spine of their oral civilization.

War Drums: Massive hide drums carried on horseback, played in precise rhythmic codes that relay battlefield orders across kilometers. Drum language is as complex as speech. A master drummer can coordinate ten thousand riders without a single spoken word.

Throat Singing: A vocal tradition in which a single singer produces multiple tones simultaneously. Throat singing requires decades of training. Masters who have practiced for two or three centuries can produce sounds that no shorter-lived singer will ever achieve---harmonics that resonate in the chest and seem to vibrate the steppe itself. Human musicians who have heard master throat singers describe the experience as physically overwhelming.

Epic Ballads: The great ballads of orcish tradition chronicle campaigns, heroes, betrayals, and clan histories. Some ballads take multiple days to perform in full. They are not sung from memory alone---they are sung by those who lived the events. A four-hundred-year-old bard singing the Ballad of the Iron River is not reciting history. They are testifying. They were there when the river ran red. They remember the names of the dead because they knew them personally.

Mourning Songs: Sung for the fallen, mourning songs accumulate verses across centuries. A mourning song for a great warrior may contain two hundred verses added by successive generations of their clan---but the first verse was composed by someone who held them as they died, and that singer may still live.

### Storytelling and Oral History

Among shorter-lived races, oral history degrades. Names blur. Details shift. Myth replaces memory.

Among the orcs, oral history is eyewitness testimony.

An elder who is four hundred years old does not tell you a story about the Great Khan's last battle. They tell you what they saw. They tell you where they stood. They tell you the Khan's exact words, because they heard them. They tell you what the sky looked like, because they were looking at it.

This fundamentally changes the nature of orcish history. There is no mythologizing. There is no "according to legend." There is an old orc sitting across the fire, saying: "I was there. This is what happened. Do not let anyone tell you otherwise."

Imperial scholars who study orcish history find this deeply unsettling. Human chronicles contradict each other across centuries. Orcish accounts from the same period are consistent---because the same witnesses are still alive to correct errors.

### Philosophy

With centuries to observe, reflect, and debate, the orcs have developed philosophical traditions of remarkable depth.

The Philosophy of the Road: The central orcish philosophical tradition holds that existence is movement. Stagnation is death. Growth is not accumulation---it is distance traveled, challenges met, horizons crossed. A life well lived is a road well ridden. An orc philosopher with three centuries of travel has seen more of the world than most human scholars will read about in a lifetime.

The Sky Dialogues: A tradition of philosophical debate conducted under open sky---never indoors, never under a roof. Two orcs pose questions to each other about duty, honor, death, and purpose. Some Sky Dialogues have continued between the same two philosophers for over a century, each meeting adding new arguments shaped by intervening decades of experience.

The Doctrine of Witnessed Truth: Orcs place absolute value on firsthand knowledge. A truth spoken by one who was present outweighs a thousand written accounts by those who were not. This is not anti-intellectual---it is a epistemological framework that privileges direct experience. When your elders live for centuries, firsthand knowledge covers vast spans of history.

The Paradox of Empire: Orcish philosophers grapple with a central contradiction---the Khanate was the greatest achievement of orcish civilization, but it was also an empire, and empires require the subjugation of the road. The orcs who rode farthest and fastest built a thing that would have eventually required them to stop riding. This paradox is debated in Sky Dialogues across the steppes. No consensus has been reached. None is expected. The debate itself is the point.

### Family and Kinship

Orcish families are vast, multi-generational structures held together by bonds that span centuries.

Clan Structure: The clan is the fundamental social unit, composed of dozens to hundreds of extended families bound by blood, marriage, oath-bonding, and shared campaign history. A clan's eldest members may be four or five hundred years old---still active, still riding, still participating in clan governance.

Generational Depth: In a human family, grandparents represent living memory. In an orc family, great-great-great-grandparents ride alongside their descendants. A child learns horsemanship from an ancestor who learned it three centuries ago. Family disputes can stretch across generations not because grudges are inherited, but because the original parties are still alive and still arguing.

Marriage and Oath-Bonding: Marriage between orcs is a bond expected to last centuries. Oath-bonding---the non-romantic binding of two warriors into a partnership of mutual obligation---is equally sacred. Both are entered with the understanding that they represent commitments measured in lifetimes, not years.

Children: Orc children are raised collectively by the clan, with specific skills taught by whichever elder possesses the greatest mastery. A child may learn riding from a two-hundred-year-old cavalry veteran, leatherwork from a three-hundred-year-old artisan, and law from a four-hundred-year-old judge. The educational depth available within a single clan is extraordinary.

Death and Mourning: Because orcs live so long, death---when it comes through violence, disease, or the rare natural end---carries immense weight. Centuries of accumulated relationships are severed. The mourning songs grow longer. The banners grow heavier. An orc who dies at four hundred has been known, personally and deeply, by hundreds of living people across multiple generations.

### Daily Life on the Steppe

What does a day look like for an orc on the steppe?

Dawn: The camp wakes with the sun. Horses are watered and checked. The herd animals---cattle, goats, sheep---are counted and moved to morning grazing. Breakfast is fermented mare's milk, dried meat, and hard cheese eaten in the saddle or around low fires.

Morning: Riders patrol the clan's current range. Scouts report on weather, game, and any movement on the horizon. Young warriors train under the supervision of century-old veterans. Craftspeople work leather, bone, and metal in portable workshops. Elders discuss route plans, clan business, and legal matters.

Midday: The clan moves if the season demands it. Gers are struck with practiced efficiency---a full camp can be packed and mounted within an hour. The herd moves. Riders flank. Children ride with their families or with designated teachers. Songs are sung during the ride. Stories are told. Philosophy is debated.

Afternoon: If the clan has settled for the day, afternoons are for training, craft, and preparation. Weapons are maintained. Horses are groomed and exercised. Meat is dried. Leather is cured. Felt is pressed. Trade goods are prepared for the next encounter with merchants or other clans.

Evening: The communal meal is the social center of orcish life. Clans gather around shared fires. Meat is roasted. Fermented drinks are passed. This is when the stories are told, the songs are sung, the philosophical debates resume, and the elders speak of what they have seen across the centuries. Children sit close and listen. This is how knowledge passes.

Night: Sentries watch. The steppe is never entirely safe. Wolves, rival clans, imperial patrols, and worse move in the dark. Those not on watch sleep in their gers, wrapped in felt and leather, listening to the wind that their ancestors heard centuries ago.

## The Modern Era

In the modern era, the orcs are fewer in number and divided into fragmented clans.

### Orc Perspective

This fragmentation is not viewed as defeat, but as dormancy.

### What Remains

- The laws still exist
- The routes are remembered
- The old commands are taught

### Living Legends

The Great Khan unified the clans forty-five years ago. By human reckoning, that is nearly two generations---distant enough for myth to replace memory.

By orcish reckoning, forty-five years is nothing.

An orc who rode with the Khan at age twenty is now sixty-five. With a lifespan of three to five centuries, they are barely past youth. They are not old veterans clinging to fading memories. They are warriors in their prime or approaching it, with centuries of life ahead of them.

These orcs are everywhere on the steppes.

| Category | Reality |
|----------|---------|
| **Veterans of the Khanate** | Thousands still live. Most are under a hundred years old. They remember every detail. |
| **The Khan's Inner Circle** | Senior commanders who served the Khan personally still ride. Some are two or three hundred years old and were already legendary before the unification. |
| **Witnesses** | Every orc over forty-five personally remembers the Khanate. They remember what unity felt like. They remember what it could do. |
| **The Dream** | Reunification remains a living memory carried by tens of thousands of orcs who were there, not legend, not myth. |

This is the fact that imperial strategists lose sleep over. Among humans, the Khanate is fading into history books. Among orcs, the warriors who built it are still in their prime, still telling their children what they saw, still carrying the old banners, still remembering the old commands.

The dream of reunification does not diminish with time. It sharpens.

## Imperial Suppression of Reunification

The orc clans have not reunited. Not for lack of will. Not for lack of leadership. Not from internal division.

The Holy Empire actively and systematically prevents orcish reunification through military force.

### The Policy

Imperial doctrine classifies any large gathering of orc clans as a threat to imperial security. This classification authorizes preemptive military intervention. The policy is not publicly declared---it is operational doctrine, communicated through military channels and enforced through standing orders to frontier legions.

### Methods of Suppression

| Method | Implementation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Population Dispersal** | Imperial legions break up orc settlements that grow beyond a threshold size. Villages that attract too many families are raided, scattered, or burned. |
| **Assembly Bans** | Large gatherings of multiple clans---for trade, for ceremony, for council---are treated as military threats. Imperial cavalry patrols disperse them by force. |
| **Leadership Targeting** | Any orc who begins consolidating authority across multiple clans is identified, tracked, and eliminated. Assassination, arrest on fabricated charges, or military strike. The empire does not allow a new Khan to rise. |
| **Structural Separation** | Imperial policy deliberately maintains division between orc clans. Trade restrictions prevent economic cooperation. Movement restrictions limit clan migration routes. Information control prevents coordinated communication. |
| **Steppe Patrols** | Imperial legions maintain permanent patrol circuits across the orcish steppes specifically to monitor for signs of clan consolidation. These are not border patrols. They are occupation forces monitoring a subject population. |
| **Hostage Systems** | Prominent orc families are pressured to send members to imperial cities as "guests" and "cultural ambassadors"---functionally hostages ensuring clan compliance. |

### Why It Works (For Now)

The empire's suppression is effective because it targets the process of unification, not the desire for it. Orcs do not lack the will to reunite. They lack the ability to gather without imperial legions descending on them.

Every orc on the steppe understands this. Every elder who remembers the Khanate knows that the empire's greatest fear is a single signal fire calling the clans together. The empire knows it too. So it ensures that signal fire is never lit.

### Why It Cannot Work Forever

The empire suppresses orc reunification through constant expenditure of military resources. This requires:
- Permanent legion deployments on the steppes
- Continuous intelligence operations
- Regular military actions that generate resentment
- Sustained political will across human generations

The orcs outlive human emperors. They outlive human generals. They outlive human policies. A suppression strategy that depends on human institutional memory faces an opponent with three to five centuries of personal memory.

Imperial generals rotate every few years. The orc elders they monitor have been watching imperial strategy evolve for centuries. They have seen every tactic the empire deploys because they have outlived every commander who deployed them.

> The empire must suppress the orcs forever.
> The orcs only need the empire to fail once.

## The Imperial Documentation System

Orcs carry papers. This is worth stating plainly because it communicates, in a single fact, the depth of imperial humiliation.

A people who once reshaped borders and shattered kingdoms now carry documents proving they are permitted to exist on land their ancestors conquered. A warrior whose great-grandmother rode with the Khan now presents a stamped parchment to a twenty-year-old human soldier at a bridge checkpoint, and waits while the soldier reads it slowly, mispronouncing the orcish name, and decides whether to let them pass.

Deliberately so. That's the point.

### What Orc Papers Contain

Orcish documentation is classified as Enhanced Non-Human Registration, the most burdensome tier applied to any race that complies with the system.

| Category | What Is Recorded |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Identity** | Name, clan affiliation, physical description, distinguishing marks (scars, tattoos, tribal markings) |
| **Status** | Transient, resident, trade visitor, labor permit holder. Orcs are never classified as "citizens." |
| **Clan History** | Which clan, which sept, which family grouping. Cross-referenced against imperial intelligence on clan political activity. |
| **Travel Authorization** | Province-specific permits required for any movement. An orc traveling from the steppes to a market town needs separate permits for departure, transit, and arrival. |
| **Military Assessment** | Whether the bearer has served in any military capacity, participated in raids, or attended multi-clan gatherings. Self-reported and verified against imperial patrol records. |
| **Renewal** | Every two years. The most frequent renewal cycle of any compliant race. |

### How the System Targets Orcs

Imperial doctrine does not formally acknowledge that the documentation system applies differently to different races. In practice, the disparity is absolute.

| Practice | Reality |
|----------|---------|
| **Checkpoint Frequency** | Orcs are stopped at every checkpoint, every time. No exceptions. No "known traveler" allowance. Papers demanded on approach, verified against registry, held during inspection. |
| **Random Inspection** | Imperial patrols on the steppes and frontier conduct "random" inspections that target orc travelers at rates five to ten times higher than any other race. |
| **Extended Verification** | Orc papers are held for "extended verification" more often than any other demographic. This can mean hours of detention at a checkpoint while a clerk "confirms" details that are already stamped and sealed. |
| **Confiscation** | Orc papers are "temporarily confiscated for administrative review" at a rate that imperial auditors, when they bother to audit, describe as "statistically notable." Papers confiscated are returned damaged, delayed, or not at all. |
| **Clan Flagging** | Certain clan affiliations trigger automatic enhanced scrutiny. Any clan that has produced a leader who attempted cross-clan coordination is flagged. This means most major clans are flagged. |
| **Gathering Restrictions** | Documentation for multi-clan gatherings requires permits that are routinely denied. When granted, the gathering is monitored by imperial observers who record the names of all attendees for future intelligence use. |

### The Orcish Response

Orcs comply. They comply visibly, resentfully, and with the controlled fury of a people who understand that the documentation system is not about safety.

It is about reminding orcs who rules.

Every checkpoint interaction is a performance. The imperial soldier asserts authority. The orc presents papers. The soldier takes longer than necessary. The orc waits. Neither speaks beyond what is required. Both understand what is happening. Both maintain the fiction that this is administration, not humiliation.

What orcs say among themselves:

> "They want us to carry proof that we are permitted to breathe. We carry it. We remember who made us carry it. We remember what happens when we stop."

> "The paper says I am allowed on this road. My grandmother's grandmother fought a war on this road. The paper means nothing. But the soldier behind it has a sword, and swords mean something until there are enough of us to take them."

> "Two years. Every two years I stand before a clerk and prove I still exist. The clerk is an elf. The elf knows this is wrong. We both say nothing."

### The Elder Perspective

Orcs who have lived for centuries carry a particular weight at checkpoints. A three-hundred-year-old orc presenting papers to a human soldier who has been alive for twenty years understands something the soldier does not: this system is temporary. The soldier was born into it and believes it is permanent. The orc has watched two previous systems of imperial control rise and collapse.

Orc elders comply with documentation requirements because they have calculated the cost of refusal and determined it is not yet time. Not because the system is legitimate. Not because they accept it. Because they are patient.

Compliance is tactical. It is not surrender.

The routes are still remembered. The old commands are still taught. And when the empire's documentation system lies in ashes alongside everything else the empire built, the orcs will still be riding.

They will not be carrying papers.

## Orc-Elf Relations

Orcs and elves regard each other with deep mutual disdain, rooted in fundamentally different responses to imperial power.

### The Orcish View of Elves

Orcs view elves as cowards who submitted to the empire voluntarily.

The orcs understand what the empire is. They have fought it. They were broken by military force---legions, cavalry, sieges, and the constant machinery of suppression. They were never offered a comfortable role within the imperial system. They were beaten, scattered, and monitored.

They did not choose submission. It was forced upon them. And they have never stopped resisting it.

When an orc---who may have personally fought imperial legions for two hundred years---looks at an elf stamping imperial permits, they see the worst kind of weakness. The elves were not conquered in the way orcs understand conquest. The elves were offered survival through compliance, and they accepted.

To orcish sensibility, this is incomprehensible. An orc who has lived three centuries and spent every one of them refusing to bow sees an elf who has lived three thousand years and spent the last five hundred processing paperwork for the civilization that destroyed their homeland. The contempt is visceral.

| Orcish Judgment | Reasoning |
|-----------------|-----------|
| **"They chose the collar."** | Elves integrated into the empire rather than fighting to the last. |
| **"They serve the hand that burned them."** | Elves administer the bureaucracy of the empire that destroyed Calidar. |
| **"Five hundred years of obedience."** | In orcish culture, five hundred years is long enough to have fought and won a dozen wars of liberation. The elves spent it filing documents. |
| **"They have ten thousand years and no spine."** | Elven longevity makes their compliance even more contemptible. They have time beyond imagining and use it to serve. |

### The Elven View of Orcs

Elves view orcs as brutish, undisciplined, and incapable of understanding the choices forced upon a conquered people.

Elves remember Calidar. They remember what the empire did with Heaven's Atlas. They made a calculated decision: compliance or extinction. The orcs, who never faced that specific calculus---total civilizational annihilation in a single day---cannot understand the weight of that choice.

Elves see orcish defiance as the luxury of a people who were defeated but not destroyed. The orcs lost a war. The elves lost a world. The difference is absolute.

| Elven Judgment | Reasoning |
|----------------|-----------|
| **"They were beaten, not erased."** | The orcs still have their steppes. Calidar is glass. |
| **"Defiance is easy when you have somewhere to retreat to."** | The orcs can scatter across the grasslands. The elves had nowhere to go. |
| **"They mistake stubbornness for honor."** | Orcish refusal to adapt is not courage---it is rigidity. |
| **"They do not understand what we endure to survive."** | Elven compliance is strategic patience across millennia, not weakness. |

### The Truth Between Them

Both positions contain validity. Both contain blind spots.

The orcs are right that elven compliance sustains the empire. Every permit stamped, every census filed, every trade tariff processed by elven hands strengthens the system that oppresses both peoples.

The elves are right that the orcs never faced the annihilation of their entire civilization in a single hour. The orc choice to resist was made from a position where resistance remained possible. The elven choice to comply was made from a position where the alternative was total extinction.

### Points of Contact

Despite the disdain, orcs and elves interact in limited ways:

- Trade: Orcish leather and bone goods pass through elven-administered markets. Business is conducted with cold professionalism.
- Imperial Courts: Orcs who enter imperial territory encounter elven bureaucrats. The interactions are terse.
- Frontier: On the edges of the steppes, where imperial control is weakest, individual orcs and elves occasionally find common ground in shared resentment of the empire. These alliances are fragile and never spoken of publicly.

## How Other Races View Orcs

| Race | Perception |
|------|------------|
| **Humans** | Fear as existential military threat. Study their past campaigns obsessively. Propaganda exaggerates savagery to justify readiness. Imperial generals consider orc reunification the single greatest strategic threat to the Holy Dominion. |
| **Elves** | View as brutish and undisciplined. Acknowledge their military history but consider orcish defiance reckless and unsophisticated. Quietly note that orcs were never offered the choice between compliance and annihilation---they were merely defeated, which is a simpler thing to endure. |
| **Gnomes** | Classify as high-mobility destabilizing forces. Quietly plan around them. Gnomish analysts respect orcish logistics and decentralized command structure as surprisingly efficient. Some production councils study orcish herd-management techniques for application to resource distribution. |
| **Dwarves** | Remember fighting them underground. Sealed tunnels forever afterward. Dwarves respect orcish self-sufficiency and their rejection of inherited privilege. Some dwarven historians note parallels between guild councils and clan councils---both systems that reject individual rule. |
| **Beast Folk** | Respect orcish mobility and understand the nomadic life from their own diasporic experience. Cat folk caravans sometimes cross orcish steppe routes and trade is conducted with mutual respect. Beast folk recognize orcs as fellow outsiders to the imperial system. "The roads belong to those who ride them"---a sentiment both peoples share. |
| **Goblins** | Respect orcish effectiveness against the empire. Some orc bands coordinate with goblin cells against imperial targets. Goblins see orcs as fellow victims of imperial suppression---the orcs were conquered; the goblins were nearly exterminated. Shared enemy creates shared purpose, though orcish honor codes and goblin insurgency tactics sometimes clash in practice. |
| **Lizard Folk** | Admire orcish discipline and martial sects. Wary of orcish directness, which contrasts sharply with lizard folk secrecy. Some lizard folk trade sects maintain quiet commercial relationships with steppe clans. The lizard folk respect any civilization that endures across centuries---and with their new lifespans, the orcs qualify. |

### Physical Traits

- **Skin Tones:** Green, grey, brown, olive-green
- **Hair Colors:** Black, dark brown, grey
- **Eye Colors:** Red, yellow, orange, brown
- **Features:** Tusks, muscular build, broad features, riders' physique

### Lifespan

- **Minimum:** 300 years
- **Maximum:** 500 years

This extraordinary longevity fundamentally shapes orcish civilization. Warriors accumulate centuries of combat experience. Artisans refine their craft across lifetimes that shorter-lived races cannot comprehend. Elders who lived through the era of the Great Khan are still alive, still riding, still remembering. A three-hundred-year-old orc is not ancient---they are experienced. A five-hundred-year-old orc has watched empires rise and fall from the saddle, and they remember every one.

1. The orcs once formed the most effective military civilization the world has ever known.
2. Under a Great Khan, unified clans reshaped borders and shattered kingdoms.
3. Orcish warfare is built on speed, intelligence, and psychological collapse.
4. They fight wars of collapse, not attrition.
5. Orcish law applies equally to all---including the Khan.
6. Every orc is raised to ride, fight, and obey command.
7. Orcs revere the sky, the road, and ancestral memory---not gods.
8. Necromancy and demons are rejected as dishonorable distortions of strength.
9. Current fragmentation is dormancy, not defeat.
10. The orcs do not need to grow stronger---they only need to unite again.
11. Human propaganda justifies constant military readiness against orcs.
12. Imperial strategists study orcish campaigns obsessively.
13. **Orcs live 300-500 years.** Veterans of the Khanate (45 years ago) are still in their prime. Living witnesses carry the dream of reunification through personal testimony, not myth.
14. **The Holy Empire actively suppresses orcish reunification** through military force, population dispersal, assembly bans, leadership targeting, and constant steppe patrols. This is the primary reason the clans remain divided.
15. **Orcs and elves view each other with mutual disdain.** Orcs see elves as cowards who chose compliance. Elves see orcs as brutes who never faced true annihilation. Both positions contain truth. Neither side concedes.
16. **Orcish culture extends far beyond warfare.** Centuries-old artisans, philosophers, throat singers, and storytellers represent a civilization of extraordinary depth---shaped by nomadic life but never limited by it.
17. **Orcish oral history is eyewitness testimony.** When an elder tells you about an event from three centuries ago, they were there. This gives orcish history an accuracy that written chronicles of shorter-lived races cannot match.
18. **The empire must suppress the orcs forever. The orcs only need the empire to fail once.**
19. **Orcs carry Enhanced Non-Human Registration papers**, the most burdensome documentation tier of any compliant race. Two-year renewal cycles, province-specific travel permits, clan affiliation tracking, and checkpoint inspections at rates far exceeding other races. Compliance is tactical, not acceptance. Every paper carried is a humiliation endured with calculated patience.
20. **"They want us to carry proof that we are permitted to breathe."** Orc elders who have lived for centuries understand the documentation system is temporary. They comply because the cost of refusal is not yet worth the price. When the time comes, they will not be carrying papers.
