# The Lizard Folk
## Keepers of the Hidden River
### Complete Lore Document

---

## Core Philosophy

The lizard folk endure through secrecy, continuity, and control of what others overlook. Knowledge, not territory, defines their survival. What is hidden endures. What is revealed can be taken.

---

## Historical Legacy

The lizard folk descend from an **ancient desert civilization** that once ruled fertile river corridors hidden deep within the sands.

### The Hidden River Empire

Their cities rose along rivers that outsiders rarely found, in fertile corridors hidden deep within the desert sands. When conditions changed, the cities vanished just as quietly. Much of the empire still exists, hidden beneath sand, stone, and silence.

Over generations, the lizard folk withdrew from open empire and turned inward. What remains today is a culture shaped by secrecy, preservation, and selective presence in the wider world.

Other races know the lizard folk as heirs to a forgotten river empire. Few understand how much of that empire still exists.

---

## Society Structure

Lizard folk society is divided into **sects** bound by ritual, lineage, and guarded knowledge.

### The Sect System

Each sect maintains control over specific traditions. No single authority governs all sects; unity is maintained through shared memory and mutual dependence. Sects rarely reveal themselves fully to outsiders. Information is shared deliberately and often incompletely.

Outsiders may encounter a caravan leader, a scholar, or a mercenary officer without ever realizing which sect they serve or what deeper obligations bind them.

---

## The Sects

Each sect guards specific knowledge domains passed down through generations.

### Known Sect Domains

- **River Engineering**: Control of hidden waterways and irrigation systems
- **Burial Rites**: Preservation of the dead and ancestral memory
- **Trade Routes**: Knowledge of desert paths and safe passages
- **Astronomy**: Celestial navigation and calendar keeping
- **Martial Discipline**: Protection of sect interests and hidden places

### Sect Secrecy

The sects do not advertise their affiliations. A lizard folk trader might serve the trade route sect. A desert guide might belong to the astronomers. A bodyguard might answer to martial discipline. You will never know unless they choose to tell you.

---

## Knowledge Over Territory

Where other civilizations claim land, the lizard folk control knowledge. Where others build visible cities, they maintain hidden ones. Where others expand borders, they preserve what exists. Where others rule openly, they influence quietly.

The lizard folk watched empires rise and fall while their hidden river cities persisted. Secrecy is not paranoia; it is proven strategy. What is hidden endures. What is revealed can be taken.

---

## The Modern Era

In the present age, lizard folk maintain a **quiet but widespread presence** across many regions of the world. While the Hidden River Territories remain their ancestral heartland, lizard folk communities exist in far more places than outsiders realize.

### Current Presence

They appear as guides with knowledge of desert paths unavailable elsewhere, traders with access to rare goods and hidden markets, engineers skilled in water management and construction. Some are simply silent observers, watching, learning, reporting to their sects.

### Settlement Patterns

- Lizard folk communities exist across the continent, in swamps, river deltas, coastal caves, and underground waterways
- Most settlements are small, secretive, and invisible to outsiders
- The **Shadow Fen** hosts a particularly significant lizard folk population, one of the largest communities outside the Hidden River Territories. The Fen's waterways, hidden channels, and resistance to imperial oversight make it an ideal environment for a people who survive through concealment
- Lizard folk presence in the Shadow Fen predates the commune itself; they were among the first to recognize the swamp's strategic value
- When lizard folk appear in cities or trade hubs, it is purposeful, but many more exist in places the empire has never mapped

### Response to Threats

When threats arise, the sects respond quietly and decisively, often without drawing attention.

## The Imperial Documentation System

Minimal exposure. Maximum concealment. Deliberate incompleteness.

### Compliance as Camouflage

When lizard folk travel through imperial territory, they carry papers. Not because they recognize imperial authority. Because carrying papers avoids attention. Avoiding attention preserves secrecy. Secrecy ensures survival.

The documentation a lizard folk trader or guide presents at an imperial checkpoint is technically valid. A name, a status classification, a travel route, the required seals. It satisfies the minimum requirements.

It reveals nothing.

The name on the papers may be a sect-assigned cover identity. The listed status, "desert trader," "guide," conceals which sect the bearer actually serves. The travel route between two known cities omits the hidden river paths, underground waterways, and sect strongholds the bearer actually visits. The papers show no magical lineage, no network connections, no hint of the sect contacts and observers operating across the same region.

### The Art of the Partial Truth

Lizard folk do not forge documents. Forgery creates risk. Detection of forgery triggers investigation. Investigation draws attention. Attention is the enemy of secrecy.

Instead, lizard folk present documents that are accurate in every verifiable detail and incomplete in every way that matters.

The name on the papers belongs to a real person. It may not belong to the person carrying them, but the name exists in imperial registries, the physical description is close enough, and the checkpoint guard who sees three lizard folk a year cannot distinguish individual variations in scale pattern.

The travel route listed is a route the bearer has plausibly traveled. It is not the route they actually took. It does not include the underground river passage, the hidden oasis, or the sect rendezvous point. Those routes exist on no imperial map and in no imperial record.

The status is accurate: desert trader. Desert guide. Traveling engineer. These are real roles that lizard folk perform. They are also covers for observation, intelligence gathering, and sect communication. The empire sees a useful service provider. The sects see an operative.

### What Lizard Folk Reveal to Imperial Authorities

Precisely as much as necessary. Not one detail more.

Lizard folk questioned beyond the standard checkpoint interaction respond with calm, unhurried answers that provide nothing of value while appearing cooperative. A lizard folk who has lived six hundred years has been questioned by imperial authorities hundreds of times. They have refined the art of saying nothing while speaking at length.

> **Checkpoint guard**: "Your travel route shows you came from the eastern desert. What business did you have there?"
> **Lizard folk**: "Water survey for a client. The wells are shifting. It happens in dry seasons."
> **Guard**: "Who was the client?"
> **Lizard folk**: "A merchant house. I do not discuss client details. Professional discretion. You understand."

The guard understands. The guard moves on. The lizard folk passes through carrying sect intelligence that would reshape imperial understanding of the desert region if anyone knew to ask the right questions.

No one asks the right questions.

### Those Without Papers

In the Hidden River Territories, in the deeper Shadow Fen communities, in the underground waterways and concealed oases, most lizard folk carry no imperial documentation at all. They exist entirely outside the imperial system.

The empire does not know this. The empire does not know how many lizard folk exist. The empire does not know where most lizard folk live. Imperial census estimates for lizard folk populations vary wildly because the sects do not share data and the empire cannot verify independently.

When an undocumented lizard folk needs to enter imperial territory, documentation is arranged through sect channels. Papers appear. They are convincing. They reference real registry entries. They will survive any standard inspection.

How the sects obtain blank imperial documentation, valid registry entries, and authentic seal patterns is a sect matter.

It is not discussed.

### The Sect Perspective

To the sects, imperial documentation is another apparatus to be navigated and rendered irrelevant. Not a source of indignation (as it is for orcs), existential threat (as it is for beast folk), or ideological battleground (as it is for goblins).

An obstacle. The sects have been managing obstacles since before the empire learned to write.

*"A document is a door. We carry the key when the door is useful. When it is not, we take a path that has no door."*

## The Hidden Empire Today

### What Still Exists

The hidden rivers still flow beneath the sands. Ancient cities remain inhabited, still hidden. Knowledge archives are maintained by dedicated sects. Trade networks operate quietly across the continent.

The lizard folk learned long ago that visible empires attract conquerors. Hidden empires endure.

## The Veiled Hand — Founded by the Observers

Five hundred years ago, a lizard folk sect tasked with **long-range observation and celestial record-keeping** witnessed the activation of Heaven's Atlas from afar.

**What they witnessed**:
- The destruction of Calidar
- Rivers vanishing from their charts
- Star alignments shifting from the magical devastation
- Magical currents collapsing into silence
- An entire civilization ending in a single recorded event

They concluded that power had escaped all restraint. Empires armed with absolute force could not be confronted through armies or diplomacy. Such powers could only be constrained through precision, patience, and removal of key individuals before catastrophe became inevitable.

From this understanding, the sect founded **The Veiled Hand**, an assassin organization dedicated to preventing escalation to mass atrocity. The guiding principle: "Preventing a single decision can save countless lives." The mission: remove those who would authorize decisions like Calidar's destruction before they can act.

The Veiled Hand operates from the Shadow Fen Commune, with the highest initiates remaining tied to the founding lizard folk sect. It exists to ensure Heaven's Atlas is never used again, or that those who would use it do not live long enough to authorize it.

## The Ancestral Waters

### The Origin Secret

The lizard folk guard their deepest secret with absolute silence:

**They did not originate on the surface.**

Their species emerged in the **Subterranean Seas**—the vast underground oceans of the hollow earth.

### The Migration

Millennia ago, lizard folk tribes followed underground rivers upward from the hollow earth to the surface world. They emerged into desert river corridors, settled, and built the Hidden River Empire. Knowledge of the migration path is guarded by specific sects.

Lizard folk are the ONLY surface race with confirmed hollow earth origins.

Their aquatic adaptations (swimming, water breathing, scaled skin) evolved for life in the Subterranean Seas—the lightless underground oceans where bioluminescent plankton illuminates black waters.

Their sect structure developed to preserve knowledge of the migration path. Specific sects maintain:
- Maps of underground river routes
- Ancestral memory of hollow earth
- Contact protocols with hollow earth kin (though contact ceased centuries ago)
- Pilgrimage rituals to ancestral waters

### The Underground Rivers

Hidden rivers beneath the desert and swamps still connect to the Subterranean Seas.

Lizard folk sects know which ones. They do not share this knowledge.

Some sects send pilgrims down the rivers to touch ancestral waters—most return. Some do not.

### Why the Silence?

The knowledge belongs to specific sects, not to outsiders. The empire denies hollow earth exists; claiming origin there invites persecution. The hidden river routes are strategic assets that would be seized if discovered. Surface lizard folk are surface now; hollow earth is ancestral, not current identity. And if the empire learned of routes to the hollow earth, they would attempt to control them.

### The Aquatic Memory

Lizard folk physiological traits make far more sense through a hollow earth lens. Their aquatic adaptations, passed off as "desert river species" traits, evolved in underground oceans. Their sensitivity to bioluminescence, dismissed as night vision, developed under the glow of deep-water plankton. Their temperature regulation, attributed to desert adaptation, originally suited geothermal waters. Even their sectarian knowledge structure, which outsiders call cultural tradition, exists to preserve migration route memory.

### The Pilgrimage

Certain high-ranking sect members undertake **the Descent**—a pilgrimage down hidden rivers to the Subterranean Seas.

Rare. Deliberate. Sacred.

Those who return speak in coded language about "touching the ancestral waters" and "seeing the first light" (bioluminescent plankton).

Those who do not return are honored as "returned to origin."

### Hollow Earth Kin

Lizard folk tribes still exist in the Subterranean Seas—the hollow earth kin who never migrated upward.

Surface and hollow lizard folk share ancestry but not culture. Millennia of separation created divergence.

Contact between surface and hollow kin is RARE and RITUALIZED. Sect elders maintain protocols, but most surface lizard folk never meet their hollow earth cousins.

### The Saurian Connection

Beyond the aquatic lizard folk kin in the Subterranean Seas, there exist the **Saurians**, intelligent dinosaur people who inhabit the Hollow Jungle, a geothermal jungle realm in the hollow earth. They are distant evolutionary cousins, something like what lizard folk were before full migration to the surface. Intelligent bipedal reptiles, builders of cities. Lizard folk sects are aware of them. Most surface peoples are not.

#### What Lizard Folk Know

Lizard folk speak obliquely of "deep cousins who never left the ancestral waters/jungles." This phrase can refer to:
- Aquatic lizard folk in Subterranean Seas (closer kin)
- Saurians in Hollow Jungle (distant cousins)

The sects do not clarify which they mean. Ambiguity is strategic.

#### Saurians as Ancestral Form

Evolutionary theory (understood by lizard folk astronomers and scholars):
- Saurians represent what lizard folk were BEFORE full adaptation to surface
- Hollow Jungle saurians remained in geothermal realm, never migrating upward
- Lizard folk migrated through underground rivers to surface deserts
- Adaptation to desert/aquatic environments created divergence

**Put simply:** Saurians are what lizard folk would be if they'd never left hollow earth.

#### The Pilgrimage to Saurian Lands

Some lizard folk sects conduct pilgrimages to the Hollow Jungle to meet saurian relatives. These journeys are extremely rare, once per generation at most. High-ranking sect members, scholars, and astronomers make the descent to honor ancestral connections, exchange knowledge, and maintain cultural memory. They find saurian cities of stone and crystal, living history. They trade surface knowledge for hollow earth wisdom.

Those who return speak in coded language: "Touched the ancestral green" or "Met the scaled ancestors who remember."

Most surface lizard folk never make this pilgrimage. Knowledge of saurians is sect-controlled.

#### Why the Silence?

Sect protocol restricts this knowledge from general discourse. The empire denies intelligent non-surface species exist, so speaking of saurians invites persecution. Revealing their cities would invite exploitation. Access to saurian knowledge and routes is a strategic asset. And the saurians chose isolation, and the lizard folk honor that choice.

#### The Cousins Who Stayed

Where lizard folk adapted and migrated, saurians remained and thrived.

Where lizard folk became desert navigators and river engineers, saurians became jungle architects and thermal ecologists.

Where lizard folk learned to navigate surface empire politics, saurians built civilizations beyond imperial reach.

Both paths are valid. Both endure.

And lizard folk remember the kinship, even when they don't speak of it.

### Access to Subterranean Seas

Breaches into the Subterranean Seas occur through:
- **Shadowfen deep dungeons** (floors 20+)
- **Hidden underground rivers** (known only to specific sects)
- **Swamp crypts** (where water meets depth)

When such breaches occur, lizard folk sects MONITOR them carefully. If empire discovers a breach, sects work to seal or obscure it.

### The Truth Beneath

Lizard folk know the hollow earth is real because they came from there. Their migration is living proof of the world beneath the world.

They will never tell the empire.
