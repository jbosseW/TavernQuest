# The Dwarves
## The Free Holds of Stone
### Complete Lore Document

---

## Core Philosophy

> **Dwarves do not believe in hierarchy. They believe in labor.**

A hold is not owned by anyone. It is maintained by everyone.

---

## Historical Legacy

The dwarves have occupied the mountain holds for millennia, predating most surface civilizations.

### The Rejection of Hierarchy

No kings. No nobles. Guild councils instead. No inherited wealth, only collective ownership. No social classes, only labor rotation. No family dynasties, only stone-born equality.

> **Labor belongs to those who give it. Stone belongs to those who work it.**

Hierarchy creates weakness and entitlement. Dwarves learned this in ages past and built accordingly.

---

## Society Structure

Dwarven society operates through **guild councils** and **collective ownership**.

### Governance

Guild councils with rotating positions. Rotation by schedule, not election, not inheritance. Decisions made through collective deliberation. No dwarf rules another.

### The Guilds

| Guild | Domain |
|-------|--------|
| **Miners** | Extraction and tunnel maintenance |
| **Smiths** | Metalworking and forging |
| **Engineers** | Construction and infrastructure |
| **Brewers** | Food, drink, and sustenance |
| **Stonecutters** | Architecture and carving |
| **Wardens** | Defense and security |

### Key Principle

> **Tasks are assigned by necessity and rotated to ensure fairness.**

Leadership positions rotate. Mastery in a craft earns respect, not privilege.

---

## Labor as Identity

Labor is the foundation of dwarven identity and ethics.

### The Value of Work

Surface peoples value wealth. Dwarves value contribution. Surface peoples value titles. Dwarves value skill. No inheritance. No land ownership. What matters is the work done.

### Labor Principles

- A dwarf's value is their contribution
- Wealth is measured in work, not gold
- All dwarves participate in labor rotation
- Every dwarf works—there is no idle class
- Hoarding is discouraged and looked down upon

> **Every dwarf who works the stone shares its bounty.**

---

## The Stone-Born

Dwarves do not reproduce biologically. They are **stone-born**.

### How New Dwarves Emerge

Asexual budding from living rock, in sacred stone chambers deep in the holds. It happens when conditions align: population need, stone quality, time. The process takes decades.

### Implications

> **No biological parents. No family lines. No inheritance disputes.**

- Every dwarf is equally a child of the stone
- No favoritism based on parentage
- No inherited rank or privilege
- All dwarves enter society as equals

---

## Conflict Resolution

Dwarves settle disputes through **council mediation**, not violence.

### The Dwarven Way

Labor disputes go to guild council arbitration. Personal conflicts get elder mediation. Serious offenses warrant full council deliberation.

### Punishment Philosophy

> **Restitution through work, not violence or exile.**

- Conflict weakens the hold
- Resolution strengthens it
- The goal is restoration, not retribution
- Even offenders contribute to the collective

---

## Religion (Or Lack Thereof)

Dwarves do not worship gods.

### What Dwarves Believe

Stone is the foundation of existence. Time is the law that governs all things. Labor is the source of meaning.

### Philosophy

> **What is built matters. What is believed does not.**

- Faith is a personal curiosity, not social foundation
- No priests, no temples, no religious hierarchy
- The stone is real. The work is real. That is enough.

---

## Surface Relations

Dwarven contact with the surface is **minimal, controlled, and deliberately restricted**. The Free Holds are **firm isolationists**. They do not participate in surface politics. They do not take sides in surface wars. They do not open their gates to surface refugees, diplomats, or armies. The mountain holds are sealed, and they intend to stay that way.

### The Sealed Gates

Every hold maintains **sealed gates**, massive stone doors that close the hold to the outside world. These gates open only for designated trade days, and only at surface trade posts built specifically for that purpose. No outsider has entered an actual dwarven hold in living memory.

Outsiders are not permitted inside the holds. Trade occurs at surface posts only. Dwarves do not sign treaties, pacts, or agreements with surface powers; they have no allies because they want none. Surface wars, famines, plagues, and political crises are surface problems. No non-dwarf has ever been granted residence in a hold. The concept does not exist. Trade provides what the holds cannot produce internally. Nothing more.

### Trade Relationship

Trade days are scheduled and infrequent. Surface peoples are customers, not partners. Not friends. Not allies. Customers. Dwarves sell metal goods, stonework, engineering consultation. Nothing strategic, nothing that reveals hold capabilities. All negotiations happen through designated trade guilds at surface posts. No outsider negotiates inside a hold. Dwarves share nothing about hold populations, layouts, defenses, or internal affairs. Ever.

### View of Surface Peoples

> **Surface hierarchies are seen as inefficient, morally suspect, and fundamentally unstable.**

Kings and lords are unnecessary complications that produce wars dwarves want no part of. Inherited privilege makes weak societies; they always collapse eventually. Surface politics spread like disease through contact. Isolation is the cure. The empire's destruction of Calidar only confirmed what dwarves always believed: surface powers cannot be trusted with anything.

### The Dwarven Stance

> **The surface can fight over crowns. The surface can burn itself to glass. Dwarves build. Dwarves endure. Dwarves do not involve themselves in the madness above.**

- **Skin Tones:** Stone-grey, pale, earth-brown
- **Hair:** Thick beards (all genders), browns, blacks, greys, occasionally reddish

## The Deep Secret

### What Surface Dwarves Do Not Say

There is a truth surface dwarves guard with collective silence: **the Deep Dwarven Realm**.

Centuries ago, not all dwarves ascended to the surface holds. Some delved deeper instead.

They found the hollow earth.

### The Schism

| Surface Dwarves | Deep Dwarves |
|-----------------|--------------|
| Sealed upper passages | Remained in the deep |
| Trade with surface | Refuse surface contact |
| Known to empire | Unknown to empire |
| Mine to moderate depths | Mine the impossible metals |
| Remember the split | Consider surface kin traitors |

### The Sealed Passages

Every surface hold contains sealed passages leading down. Guild wardens guard them. No dwarf speaks of them to outsiders.

The passages were sealed during **The Deep Schism** centuries ago—a fundamental disagreement about whether dwarves should maintain surface contact or isolate completely.

Surface dwarves chose limited engagement. Deep Dwarves chose absolute isolation.

### Deep Dwarven Realm

The Deep Dwarves built cities in the hollow earth that surpass surface architecture in age and grandeur:

- Halls kilometers long
- Bridges spanning chasms that should not exist
- Forge-temples powered by geothermal vents
- Metals unknown to surface science

They maintain the old ways—purer guild systems, stricter labor rotation, deeper stone communion.

And they consider surface dwarves **traitors who abandoned the deep**.

### Access Points

Very few breaches connect to the Deep Dwarven Realm:

1. **Sealed passages in surface holds** (guarded, never opened)
2. **Deep mines** (floors 28+, heavily suppressed when discovered)
3. **The Volcanic Descent** (Ashen Archipelago, floor 30 GUARANTEED breach)

Surface dwarves who discover breaches seal them immediately and deny their existence.

### Why the Silence?

Surface dwarves maintain absolute secrecy about Deep Dwarves. Internal matters stay internal. Guild protocol. The empire must not learn about hollow earth access. And despite the schism, Deep Dwarves are still dwarves. You protect kin.

There is also shame. Deep Dwarves are right about one thing: surface dwarves DID compromise.

### The Metals

Deep Dwarves possess metals that do not exist on the surface:

- **Voidsteel** - Absorbs magic naturally
- **Coregold** - Never tarnishes, conducts heat impossibly
- **Depthiron** - Stronger than surface iron by magnitude

Surface dwarves occasionally acquire samples through sealed passage exchanges (never acknowledged publicly). These metals appear in the finest dwarven artifacts—their origin never explained.

### The Truth

If you ask a dwarf about Deep Dwarves, you will receive silence.

If you ask about sealed passages, you will be told they're "old mining works, unstable."

If you ask about impossible metals, you will be told "trade secret, guild matter."

But in the deepest mines, on the longest night shifts, older dwarves sometimes whisper:

> **"They delved too deep. Or perhaps we didn't delve deep enough."**

The schism remains unhealed.

The passages remain sealed.

And the Deep Dwarves remember.
