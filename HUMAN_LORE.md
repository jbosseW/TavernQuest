## THE HUMANS --- THE HOLY DOMINION OF MAN

Humans are not a civilization. They are a regime.

The Holy Dominion of Man is the dominant political, military, and religious force in the known world. It occupies the fertile heartland of the main continent, controls the largest standing army, administers the most extensive bureaucratic apparatus, and enforces its will through divine mandate, institutional terror, and the simple, brutal fact that it destroyed anyone who seriously opposed it.

Five hundred years ago, the Holy Dominion deployed Heaven's Atlas and turned the elven homeland of Calidar into a glass desert. Millions died. The war ended. The Dominion declared itself the instrument of Helios's will and set about building an empire on the ashes.

Everything that follows must be understood through this lens: humans believe they are the chosen people of Helios, and all other races are lesser beings to be managed, tolerated, or eliminated as needed. Taught to children. Reinforced in law. The way a human merchant looks at an elf and sees a useful servant — this is not prejudice he feels guilty about. He does not notice it any more than he notices breathing.

The Holy Dominion exists because humans need to be in control. Not want. Need. Compulsive, institutional, total.

## The Theology of Supremacy

### The Doctrine of the Chosen

The theological framework of human supremacy is called the Doctrine of the Chosen and it permeates every level of imperial society.

Core tenets, as recorded in the Dominion Catechism:

| Tenet | Teaching |
|-------|----------|
| **Divine Selection** | Helios chose humans above all other races to carry the light of civilization |
| **The Civilizing Mission** | Humans have a sacred obligation to bring order to lesser peoples |
| **The Hierarchy of Creation** | All beings exist on a divine ladder; humans are closest to Helios, all others descend below |
| **The Sanctity of Dominion** | Imperial territory is holy ground, blessed by Helios's light |
| **The Burden of Stewardship** | Ruling other races is not privilege but duty; it is hard, thankless, necessary work |
| **The Lesson of Calidar** | The destruction of Calidar was divine judgment against those who defied Helios's order |

The catechism is one thing. Daily life is another. A landlord who charges his elf tenant double does not think of it as Hierarchy of Creation — he thinks of it as what everyone does. A woman who steps aside for a human stranger but not an orcish one has never read a tenet in her life. The doctrine does not need to be quoted to be obeyed; it just needs to be the water you grew up swimming in.

### How the Doctrine Spreads

**To children:**
- From age five, human children attend temple schools where they learn the catechism of Helios alongside reading and arithmetic
- The first prayer a child memorizes: "Helios above, who chose our kind, grant us strength to bear the burden of your light"
- History lessons frame every other race as a problem that humans solved: elves who practiced dangerous magic, orcs who brought war, goblins who infested the land
- Children are taught that kindness to lesser races is commendable, but trust in them is foolish
- The glass desert of Calidar is presented as a cautionary tale about what happens when non-humans wield power unchecked

**In law:**
- Non-humans require documentation to travel, work, reside, and exist within imperial territory
- Human testimony outweighs non-human testimony in imperial courts (ratio varies by race)
- Property ownership by non-humans is restricted and taxed at higher rates
- Interracial marriage is not technically illegal but is socially devastating and bureaucratically punished
- The Luminary Inquest has broader powers of search, seizure, and detention for non-humans

**In daily life:**
- Separate market hours for non-human merchants in some cities
- Residential districts informally segregated (formally "zoned for administrative efficiency")
- Non-human workers paid less for identical labor, justified as "adjusted for civilizational contribution"
- Social gatherings rarely mix races except in service contexts (non-humans serve, humans are served)
- Casual slurs and dehumanizing language are so normalized they barely register

### The Genuine Believers vs. The Cynics

The split that matters most in human society:

**The genuine believers** (majority of the population):
- Farmers, artisans, small merchants, soldiers, low-ranking priests
- They truly believe Helios chose humanity
- Their faith provides real comfort: meaning in hardship, community in isolation, hope in despair
- They are not evil people; they are people raised in a system that tells them they are inherently superior
- Most have never meaningfully interacted with another race as an equal
- When confronted with suffering they cause, they experience genuine cognitive dissonance
- Many perform small kindnesses across racial lines while maintaining the overall framework of supremacy

**The cynics** (leadership, high clergy, senior military, wealthy merchants):
- They know the theology is a control mechanism
- Some believe in Helios but not in the Doctrine of the Chosen
- Some believe in nothing at all but find the system profitable
- They maintain the religious facade because it provides social cohesion, justifies expansion, and prevents the population from questioning imperial authority
- The gap between public piety and private behavior is enormous
- Corruption at the top is systemic, not exceptional

> Most of the Dominion's citizens are decent people trapped in a monstrous system they genuinely believe is righteous.

## Helios Worship as Lived Experience

### The Faith of Ordinary Believers

For most humans, Helios is not an abstraction. He is a presence in their daily lives---as real as the sun that rises each morning.

**Morning devotion** (practiced by most households):
- Wake before dawn
- Face east toward the rising sun
- Recite the Dawn Prayer: "Helios ascends. We rise with Him. By His light we see the truth of our purpose."
- Light a small candle or oil lamp (in poorer homes, simply open the shutters)
- The family eats together after prayer; meals taken before dawn prayer are considered impious

**Midday observance** (practiced by the devout):
- Brief pause at noon when the sun is highest
- Silent prayer of gratitude: "At the height of Your arc, we stand in Your fullest light"
- Some workers simply touch their forehead and glance skyward
- Temple bells ring at noon in all imperial cities

**Evening reflection** (practiced by most households):
- As the sun sets, a brief prayer of thanks for the day's labor
- "Helios descends. We rest in His shadow. In darkness we remember His light."
- Children are taught that nighttime is when Helios battles darkness beneath the world, and dawn proves He always wins

### What Faith Feels Like

To a devout human, Helios worship answers every need that matters. Meaning: "We are not random. We were chosen. Our suffering has purpose." Community: weekly temple gatherings are the center of social life in every town, village, and city district. Morality: clear rules about right and wrong, backed by divine authority. Comfort in death: "The faithful ascend to Helios's eternal light." Identity: "I am human. I am chosen. I matter." And when hardship strikes---famine, plague, war---the answer is always ready: "Helios tests us to strengthen us" or "This suffering serves His plan."

This is real faith. Not performed. Most humans experience genuine spiritual comfort, genuine community, genuine moral guidance from their religion. That is what makes the system more stable---and more dangerous---than cynical manipulation alone could ever be.

### Temple Architecture

Temples of Helios follow strict architectural codes:

**Exterior:**
- Always the tallest building in any settlement (law requires this)
- White stone or whitewashed walls (representing Helios's light)
- Gold leaf on domes, spires, and window frames
- No shadows should fall on the entrance during daylight hours (entrances face east)
- Grand Cathedral in the capital is visible for miles, its golden dome catching the sun

**Interior:**
- Central nave designed so sunlight streams through eastern windows at dawn
- No permanent candles during daytime; natural light is the divine presence
- At night, hundreds of candles and oil lamps create a golden glow
- The altar is always at the eastern end, raised, with a golden sun disc behind it
- No seats in traditional temples (worshippers stand or kneel; sitting is for the weak)
- Newer, reformed temples have benches (considered decadent by traditionalists)

**The Grand Cathedral:**
- Seat of the High Luminary and religious authority
- Built directly above Helios's actual prison (though only the Keeper knows this)
- Underground levels extend far below the public structure
- The deepest chamber has not been seen by more than one living person at a time in five centuries

### Festival Days

| Festival | Season | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **The Ascension** | Spring Equinox | Celebrates Helios's triumph over winter darkness. Bonfires, feasts, public pardons for minor crimes. The most joyful holiday. |
| **The Radiance** | Summer Solstice | The longest day. Continuous prayers from dawn to dusk. Fasting until sunset, then enormous communal feasts. Marriages performed at noon are considered especially blessed. |
| **The Waning** | Autumn Equinox | A solemn day acknowledging that darkness grows. Charity to the poor is expected. Offerings of harvest goods to temples. |
| **The Vigil** | Winter Solstice | The longest night. All-night prayer vigils. Every candle in the city is lit. The faithful stay awake to "keep Helios company in His darkest hour." Dawn brings weeping relief and celebration. |
| **Victory Day** | Anniversary of Calidar | Celebrates the end of the Last World War. Military parades. Sermons about the cost of allowing "chaos magic" to threaten civilization. Elves are expected to attend and express gratitude for their "salvation through integration." |
| **Integration Day** | Anniversary of elven incorporation | Public celebration of "unity." Elves perform public loyalty oaths. Human citizens are encouraged to "welcome our elven brothers and sisters." The most uncomfortable holiday for everyone involved. |

### The Priesthood

**Structure:**

| Rank | Role | Number |
|------|------|--------|
| **The High Luminary** | Supreme religious authority, commander of the Inquest | 1 |
| **Solar Cardinals** | Senior clergy governing major regions | 12 |
| **Flame Bishops** | City-level religious leaders | ~200 |
| **Sun Priests** | Temple-level clergy, conduct services and rites | ~5,000 |
| **Dawn Acolytes** | Junior clergy, assistants, students | ~15,000 |
| **Lay Brothers/Sisters** | Volunteers who serve temples without ordination | ~50,000 |

**Ordination:**
- Priests are ordained through a ritual called the Illumination, where they stand in direct sunlight and are "filled with Helios's fire"
- The ritual takes an entire day, dawn to dusk, standing without food or water
- Those who collapse are considered "not yet ready" and may try again the following year
- Cynics note that this selects for physical endurance, not spiritual devotion

**Divine magic:**
- Helios-ordained priests channel what is officially called "divine light"
- Healing, blessing, purification, and holy combat are the recognized schools
- This magic is exempt from the general magic ban because it flows "from Helios, not from mortal will"
- The magic still works in the present day, 500 years after the war
- Whether this is genuinely divine, residual energy from Helios's imprisoned form, or something else entirely is one of the deepest mysteries of the world (see: The Helios Revelation)

## Ordinary Imperial Life

### The Class Structure

Human society is rigidly stratified, despite the theological claim that all humans are "equal before Helios."

| Class | Population | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| **The Imperial Court** | <0.1% | The Emperor, immediate family, highest advisors. Near-mythical figures rarely seen in public. |
| **The Nobility** | ~2% | Landed families controlling provinces, cities, and trade routes. Hereditary privilege maintained through complex genealogical law. |
| **The Clergy** | ~3% | Priests, Inquest officers, temple administrators. Significant overlap with nobility. |
| **The Military Officer Class** | ~2% | Commanders, strategists, garrison leaders. Often younger sons of noble families. |
| **The Merchant Class** | ~8% | Wealthy traders, guild leaders, master artisans. Can purchase social status but never true nobility. |
| **The Professional Class** | ~10% | Lawyers, physicians, engineers, senior bureaucrats. Education-based status. |
| **The Common People** | ~65% | Farmers, laborers, soldiers, minor artisans, servants. The backbone of the empire. |
| **The Underclass** | ~10% | Urban poor, itinerant workers, beggars, criminals. Helios worship is their only source of dignity. |

### Regional Variations

The Holy Dominion is not monolithic. Regional cultures vary significantly.

| Region | Character | Notable Traits |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **The Imperial Capital** | Urban, cosmopolitan, politically intense | Highest concentration of elven bureaucrats. Most diverse population. Tightest surveillance. |
| **The Northern Provinces** | Agricultural, conservative, pious | Closest to dwarven borders. Suspicious of outsiders. Strongest Helios devotion. Simple food, hard work, deep faith. |
| **The Western Marches** | Militarized, frontier mentality | Border with orcish steppes. Garrison towns. Soldiers and their families. Constant low-grade anxiety about orc reunification. |
| **The Southern Districts** | Mixed human-elf, bureaucratic | Resettled elven populations. Highest literacy rates. Complex social dynamics between human overlords and elven administrators. |
| **The Eastern Coast** | Maritime, mercantile, worldly | Trading ports facing the Shimmering Sea. Most contact with gnomish goods. Wealthiest merchant families. Most tolerant of other races (because tolerance is profitable). |
| **The Southwestern Border** | Swampy, poor, neglected | Nearest to Shadow Fen. Imperial authority weakest here. Smuggling, desertion, and quiet sympathy for the commune. |

### What Humans Eat

**Common foods:**
- Wheat bread (the staple of imperial life; bread quality indicates class)
- Root vegetables: turnips, carrots, parsnips, onions
- Preserved meats: salted pork, dried beef, smoked fish
- Cheese (dozens of regional varieties; a source of genuine cultural pride)
- Seasonal fruits: apples, pears, plums, berries
- Beer and cider (consumed daily by all classes; water is often unsafe)
- Wine (upper classes; regional vineyards compete fiercely)

**Festival foods:**
- Sun bread (golden-crusted bread baked in sun-shaped molds for holy days)
- Honeyed meats (glazed roasts served at Radiance feasts)
- Vigil soup (thick stew eaten during Winter Solstice to sustain all-night prayer)

**Class markers:**
- Nobility: Imported spices, white flour bread, fresh meat daily, southern wines
- Common people: Dark bread, root stews, salted fish, local beer
- Military rations: Hardtack, dried meat, field-brewed ale
- The very poor: Whatever they can find. Temple charity kitchens serve thin porridge.

### Family Structure

**The ideal imperial family:**
- Father as head of household (reflecting Helios as head of creation)
- Mother as keeper of the home and children's moral education
- Children raised in temple schools from age five
- Extended family provides social safety net
- Three to five children considered ideal; large families are a sign of Helios's favor

**Reality:**
- Many families are broken by military service, Inquest actions, or poverty
- Single mothers are common in garrison towns (fathers deployed or dead)
- Orphanages run by the temple are overcrowded and underfunded
- In urban areas, both parents often work; children raised by grandparents or older siblings
- Family structures in the southwestern border regions are less rigid, influenced by proximity to Shadow Fen

**Marriage:**
- Arranged marriages common among nobility and merchant class
- Common people generally marry for affection, with family approval
- Marriage is a religious ceremony performed by Sun Priests
- Divorce technically requires temple approval and is socially devastating
- Interracial marriages are legal but result in loss of social status, property restrictions, and Inquest scrutiny

### Education

**Temple schools** (ages 5-12):
- Catechism of Helios (theology and moral instruction)
- Reading and writing in Common
- Arithmetic
- Imperial history (heavily propagandized)
- Basic geography (imperial perspective only)
- Racial hierarchy (presented as natural and divine)

**Guild apprenticeships** (ages 12-16):
- Practical training in a trade
- Most common people follow this path
- Apprentices live with master craftsmen and learn through labor

**Imperial academies** (ages 12-18):
- For nobility and wealthy merchant families
- Advanced theology, law, military strategy, rhetoric, natural philosophy
- Some academies accept exceptional commoners on scholarship (rare)
- Produces the professional and officer classes

**The Seminary** (ages 16+):
- Training for the priesthood
- Theology, divine magic practice, Inquest procedures, administrative law
- The most prestigious educational track; graduates enter the clergy or Inquest

**What is NOT taught:**
- Pre-war history from non-human perspectives
- The actual causes of the Last World War
- Anything about Calidar beyond "elves wielded dangerous magic and had to be stopped"
- Non-human languages (learning them is considered suspicious)
- Critical thinking about imperial ideology

### Entertainment

| Form | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Theater** | State-approved plays glorifying imperial history. Comedies mocking non-humans are popular. Tragedies about the war emphasize human sacrifice and divine destiny. |
| **Music** | Hymns dominate public music. Tavern songs are bawdy and apolitical. Protest music exists underground (influence from goblin resistance songs, though no human would admit it). |
| **Sports** | Wrestling, archery contests, horse racing. Military-adjacent activities are most prestigious. Gambling on sports is officially discouraged, practically universal. |
| **Taverns** | The true social center of common life. Beer, conversation, gossip, occasional brawls. Taverns near military garrisons are rougher. Taverns in merchant districts are political. |
| **Festivals** | Religious holidays provide the primary entertainment calendar. Feasts, processions, fireworks (licensed), public performances. |
| **Gambling** | Officially frowned upon. Actually pervasive. Card games, dice, betting on fights. Cat folk-run gambling establishments are popular and socially transgressive. |

## The Regime

### The Emperor

The Emperor of the Holy Dominion rules by divine mandate of Helios.

**What the public knows:**
- The Emperor is Helios's chosen instrument on earth
- Imperial decrees carry divine authority
- The Emperor appears at major festivals (always at a distance, always in golden regalia)
- Portraits depict a stern, ageless figure wreathed in light

**What insiders know:**
- The Emperor governs through delegation. Daily decisions are made by advisors, ministers, and the High Luminary
- The current Emperor inherited the position through bloodline, not divine selection
- Imperial power is institutional, not personal; the Emperor could be replaced and the system would continue
- Whether the Emperor genuinely believes in Helios or merely maintains the structure is unknown even to close advisors

**The line of succession:**
- Hereditary through the eldest child (gender irrelevant since the Third Dynasty reform 200 years ago)
- If the line fails, the Solar Cardinals select a new dynasty (this has happened twice in 500 years)
- Assassination of the Emperor is the most serious crime imaginable; the punishment extends to the perpetrator's entire extended family

### The Luminary Inquest

The Luminary Inquest is the enforcement arm of both church and state. It answers to the High Luminary, who answers to the Emperor.

**Official mandate:**
- Enforce the magic ban
- Hunt vampires, demons, and forbidden beings
- Verify documentation and identity
- Investigate heresy and sedition
- Maintain public order through visible presence

**Actual function:**
- Secret police with religious authority
- Surveillance network spanning the empire
- Interrogation and detention without trial for non-humans and suspected mages
- Public executions as deterrent
- Intelligence gathering on foreign powers
- Internal political enforcement (suppressing dissent within the empire)

**Structure:**

| Division | Function |
|----------|----------|
| **The White Cloaks** | Frontline enforcers. Visible presence in cities and on roads. Conduct documentation checks, raids, and arrests. |
| **The Seekers** | Specialized mage-hunters. Trained to detect and neutralize magical practitioners. |
| **The Scribes** | Administrative division. Manage the documentation system, process reports, maintain files on suspects. |
| **The Shadows** | Intelligence division. Informant networks, surveillance operations, infiltration of suspect organizations. Not officially acknowledged. |
| **The Flame Wardens** | Execute sentences. Conduct public burnings, ritual soul destructions, and "cleansing" operations. |

**The Documentation System:**
- Every human citizen carries identity papers issued at birth
- Papers include name, parentage, place of birth, profession, tax status, and religious standing
- Non-humans require additional documentation: travel permits, work permits, residence permits, and periodic re-registration
- Failure to produce papers on demand is grounds for detention
- Falsified papers are punishable by death
- The system is administered primarily by elven bureaucrats (who understand its weaknesses better than anyone)

### The Imperial Documentation System: Bureaucracy as Control

The Imperial Documentation System is the empire's second religion. Where Helios provides spiritual authority, documentation provides material authority. Together they form an interlocking cage: one for the soul, one for the body.

**What the papers contain:**

Every being within imperial borders is required to carry documentation that records:

| Category | Information Required |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Identity** | Full name, parentage, place of birth, physical description, distinguishing marks |
| **Status** | Citizen, subject, resident, transient, refugee, indentured, or probationary |
| **Legal Standing** | Criminal record, outstanding warrants, judicial history, Inquest investigation status |
| **Magical Lineage** | Whether the individual has magical ability, magical ancestry, or any recorded connection to arcane practice. Bloodline records trace magical heritage back three generations minimum. |
| **Travel History** | Where the bearer has been, where they are going, which checkpoints they passed, when, and under what authority |
| **Religious Standing** | Temple attendance records, tithe compliance, sacramental history |
| **Economic Status** | Profession, tax classification, guild membership, debts, property |

**When papers must be presented:**

- At all checkpoints: city gates, bridge crossings, provincial borders, harbor entries
- During random inspections by patrolling White Cloaks (imperial enforcers)
- When demanded by the Luminary Inquest (any division, any time, any reason or no reason)
- When entering any imperial facility, market, public building, temple, or government office
- When conducting any commercial transaction above a trivial threshold
- When applying for any permit, license, or official document (documentation to get documentation)
- When detained, questioned, investigated, or accused

**The racial gradient:**

Not all papers are created equal. The documentation burden scales with the empire's racial hierarchy:

| Race | Documentation Burden |
|------|---------------------|
| **Humans** | Standard identity papers. Issued at birth. Renewed every ten years. Simple, straightforward, rarely questioned. |
| **Elves** | Standard papers plus magical lineage disclosure. Elves process their own documentation, which creates a strange loop of self-surveillance. Renewal every five years. |
| **Orcs** | Enhanced documentation. Travel permits required for any movement between provinces. Papers checked more frequently than any other race. Renewal every two years. |
| **Beast Folk** | Maximum documentation burden. Travel permits, city-entry permits, residence permits, trade permits. Must re-register in every city entered. Papers "lost" by authorities with suspicious frequency. |
| **Lizard Folk** | Standard non-human documentation. Rarely encountered by imperial checkpoints due to remote territories. When they do appear, their minimal paperwork draws suspicion. |
| **Gnomes** | Special diplomatic permits at Clockwork Harbor only. Gnomes within imperial borders (rare) carry trade representative credentials issued by production councils. |
| **Dwarves** | Trade permits only, issued at surface trade posts. Inside the holds, no imperial documentation exists or is recognized. |
| **Goblins** | No documentation. Goblins refuse the system entirely. Carrying no papers is itself a criminal offense, which means every goblin is a criminal from the moment of birth. This is by design. |

**The human experience:**

Most humans view the documentation system as natural and necessary. They were born into it. Their parents carried papers. Their grandparents carried papers. The idea that a person could exist without documentation is as foreign to most imperial citizens as the idea that the sun might not rise.

Common phrases:
- "Papers keep us safe."
- "If you have nothing to hide, you have nothing to fear."
- "The system works. Look at the peace we have."
- "Imagine if anyone could just go anywhere, do anything, without being tracked. Chaos."

**What the system actually does:**

The documentation apparatus is a control mechanism disguised as bureaucracy.

| Stated Purpose | Actual Purpose |
|----------------|----------------|
| "Public safety" | Population tracking and movement restriction |
| "Crime prevention" | Preemptive identification of undesirables |
| "Efficient governance" | Racial stratification through administrative burden |
| "Orderly commerce" | Economic control of non-human populations |
| "Magical security" | Surveillance of magical bloodlines for Inquest targeting |
| "Travel management" | Restriction of assembly, movement, and communication |

The genius of the system is that it requires no overt violence to function. A denied permit accomplishes what a sword cannot: it erases a person from legal existence. Without papers, you cannot work, trade, travel, rent shelter, buy food in regulated markets, or appeal to any court. You become a non-person. The system does not need to kill you. It simply makes living impossible.

**Who benefits:**

- Human citizens with clean records experience the system as invisible infrastructure. Papers are checked, stamped, returned. Minor inconvenience. Life continues.
- Non-humans experience it as a daily reminder of their subordinate status. Every checkpoint, every inspection, every demand for papers reinforces the hierarchy.
- The wealthy can purchase expedited processing, favorable reviews, and "lost" records
- The powerful are rarely stopped at all

**Who suffers:**

- Beast folk, whose nomadic patterns generate the most scrutiny and the most "lost" papers
- Orcs, who are stopped and searched at rates far exceeding any other race
- Anyone with magical lineage, whose bloodline records follow them like a curse
- The poor of all races, who cannot afford documentation fees, renewal costs, or expedited processing
- Goblins, who are criminalized for existing

**Administration:**

The documentation system is maintained by the Scribes division of the Luminary Inquest. But the actual day-to-day processing, filing, stamping, and record-keeping is performed overwhelmingly by **elven bureaucrats**.

The apparatus of imperial control is operated by the very population it was designed to surveil. Elves process the papers. Elves file the records. Elves maintain the archives.

And elves know every weakness, every loophole, every way to delay a file, lose a record, or create a clerical error that takes years to resolve.

The empire built a surveillance machine and handed the controls to the people it surveils. Whether this is oversight or unavoidable necessity depends on who you ask. The result is the same: the documentation system is only as reliable as the elves who operate it choose to make it.

### Provincial Governance

The empire is divided into provinces, each governed by an appointed **Lord Governor**.

**Governor responsibilities:**
- Tax collection and remittance to the capital
- Maintaining roads and infrastructure
- Providing military levies when demanded
- Enforcing the magic ban and supporting Inquest operations
- Administering justice through provincial courts

**Governor realities:**
- Most governors are drawn from noble families with political connections
- Corruption is systemic; governors skim taxes, sell appointments, and accept bribes
- Competition between governors creates regional rivalries that the Emperor quietly exploits
- Governors in border provinces have more autonomy (and more anxiety)
- The southwestern border governorship is considered a punishment posting

### The Military

**Standing army:**
- Approximately 150,000 professional soldiers (the Imperial Legion)
- Provincial militias add another 100,000-200,000 during wartime
- Organized into legions of 5,000, further divided into cohorts and centuries
- Officer corps drawn primarily from nobility; common soldiers from conscription and voluntary enlistment

**Doctrine:**
- Heavy infantry supported by cavalry, archers, and siege engines
- Defensive strategy: hold territory, garrison borders, project strength
- The empire fights wars of attrition, not wars of maneuver (the opposite of orcish doctrine)
- Navy protects eastern coast but has never successfully crossed to the Gnomish Isles

**The Orc Problem:**
- The western army exists primarily to deter orcish reunification
- Border forts staffed year-round with the empire's most experienced troops
- Military exercises simulate orcish mobile warfare scenarios
- Imperial generals study Khan Urzog's campaigns obsessively
- The western command knows they cannot defeat a unified Khanate in open battle; their strategy is to prevent unification through diplomacy, bribery, and assassination

**The Goblin Quagmire:**
- Suppressing goblin resistance consumes enormous resources for negligible gain
- Soldiers deployed to anti-goblin operations develop low morale and high desertion rates
- The military leadership privately considers goblin suppression unwinnable
- Publicly, every cleared warren is celebrated as a victory. Privately, generals count the cost.

### The Tax System

**Direct taxes:**
- Land tax (primary revenue source; paid by landowners based on assessed value)
- Head tax (flat rate per adult; devastating for the poor)
- Trade tax (percentage of commercial transactions)
- Non-human surcharge (additional tax on all non-human economic activity)

**Indirect taxes:**
- Tariffs on goods entering or leaving provinces
- Temple tithes (technically voluntary, socially mandatory)
- Military levies (in lieu of service, wealthy families pay to exempt sons)
- Documentation fees (every permit, registration, and renewal costs money)

**How it actually works:**
- Tax collectors are among the most hated people in the empire
- Corruption means the capital receives perhaps 60% of what is owed
- Elven administrators manage the actual accounting (and understand every loophole)
- The poorest pay proportionally the most
- The temple is tax-exempt, making it the wealthiest institution in the empire

### The Legal System

**Courts:**
- Imperial courts for serious crimes (treason, murder, magic violations)
- Provincial courts for civil and criminal matters
- Temple courts for religious offenses and marriage disputes
- Military courts for soldiers (expedited, less due process)

**Key principles:**
- Human testimony is weighted more heavily than non-human testimony
- Property crimes against humans by non-humans carry triple penalties
- Magic use without license is treason (death and soul destruction)
- The Inquest can detain without trial for up to one year ("investigation period")
- Confession obtained through "vigorous questioning" (torture) is admissible
- Noble defendants can request trial by their peers (other nobles); commoners cannot

**Punishments:**
- Fines and restitution (minor crimes)
- Public flogging (common crimes)
- Imprisonment with hard labor (serious crimes)
- Public execution by hanging (murder, theft of significant value)
- Public execution by burning (heresy, sedition, unsanctioned magic)
- Ritual soul destruction (the ultimate punishment: the convicted's soul is destroyed, preventing any afterlife)

### How Dissent Is Handled

**Levels of response:**

| Level | Dissent Type | Response |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **1 - Grumbling** | Complaining about taxes, corruption, or local officials | Ignored. Universal and harmless. |
| **2 - Questioning** | Publicly questioning imperial policy or Helios doctrine | Noted by informants. Subject placed on watch lists. |
| **3 - Organizing** | Forming groups to advocate for change | Infiltrated by Shadows division. Leaders identified and monitored. |
| **4 - Agitating** | Spreading anti-imperial sentiment or sympathizing with non-humans | Arrest. Interrogation. Public confession or disappearance. |
| **5 - Rebellion** | Armed resistance or aiding enemies of the empire | Execution. Family investigated. Community punished. |

**The informant network:**
- The Inquest maintains informants in every major city
- Neighbors are encouraged to report suspicious behavior
- Rewards for information leading to arrests
- The culture of surveillance means most people self-censor
- Trust is conditional, even among friends; political conversations happen only in private

## Relations with Other Races

### Elves: Useful Servants, Never Equals

**Official position:** "Our elven brothers and sisters have been welcomed into the empire and serve with distinction in administrative roles."

**Actual position:** Elves are the empire's most valuable possession. They run the bureaucracy, manage the archives, process the documents, and keep the machinery of empire functioning. Without them, imperial administration would collapse within a generation.

**The contradiction:**
- Humans need elves more than they will ever admit
- Elves are given responsibility without autonomy
- Elven competence is simultaneously relied upon and resented
- Promotion of elves to visible positions is framed as generosity; denial of higher positions is framed as prudence
- The Inquest monitors elven communities closely because humans suspect (correctly) that elves document everything

**What humans say about elves:**
- "Good workers. Reliable. Just don't trust them with anything truly important."
- "They're grateful for what we've given them. Mostly."
- "An elf never forgets. That's what makes them useful. And dangerous."
- "Integration was the best thing that ever happened to them. We saved them from themselves."

**What humans don't say:**
- "Without the elves, we couldn't run this empire."
- "Some of them are smarter than us. Most of them, probably."
- "They remember Calidar. They'll always remember Calidar."

### Orcs: The Existential Threat

**Official position:** "Savage barbarians who must be kept divided and contained."

**Actual position:** The single greatest military threat to the empire's existence. Imperial strategists know that a reunified Khanate would be virtually unbeatable in open battle. Every aspect of western military policy is designed to prevent this.

**Methods of containment:**
- Propaganda depicting orcs as mindless brutes (conceals genuine respect for their military capability)
- Diplomatic efforts to maintain clan divisions (bribery, playing clans against each other)
- Military exercises specifically designed to counter orcish mobile warfare
- Covert operations: assassination of potential unifiers, disruption of inter-clan gatherings
- Economic pressure: controlling trade routes to make clans dependent on imperial goods

**What humans say about orcs:**
- "Savages. Strong but stupid. That's why they'll never unite again."
- "My grandfather fought orcs on the border. Said they were the most terrifying thing he ever faced."
- "The generals say we're prepared. The generals have been saying that for 500 years."

**What imperial strategists actually think:**
- "If they unite, we have maybe three months before they reach the capital."
- "Their doctrine is superior to ours in every way that matters."
- "We cannot beat them. We can only keep them apart."

### Dwarves: Irritating Isolationists

**Official position:** "Valuable trading partners who unfortunately resist the benefits of imperial civilization."

**Actual position:** The dwarves sit on resources the empire desperately wants---rare metals, gems, engineering expertise---and refuse to provide them on imperial terms. Their mountain holds are unconquerable. Their collectivist society is ideologically offensive (no hierarchy, no religion, no private property). The empire tolerates them because the alternative is catastrophic mountain warfare for uncertain gain.

**The trade relationship:**
- Dwarves sell finished metal goods and stonework
- The empire sells grain, textiles, and surface goods
- Trade is conducted through fortified gates on dwarven terms
- The empire has repeatedly tried to negotiate exclusive access to dwarven mines; every attempt has failed
- Dwarven goods are the finest in the world; human smiths resent this

**What humans say about dwarves:**
- "Stubborn little rock-huggers. Good steel, though."
- "They don't have kings, don't worship gods, and think private property is immoral. Strange folk."
- "At least they keep to themselves. Better than orcs."
- "I heard they emerge from stone instead of being born. Unnatural."

### Goblins: Vermin to Be Exterminated

**Official position:** "Pest control. Not a military operation."

**Actual position:** The goblin resistance is an open wound that will not heal. Five hundred years of suppression, extermination campaigns, collective punishment, and bounties have accomplished nothing. The goblins refuse to die, refuse to submit, and refuse to stop attacking imperial infrastructure.

**The imperial perspective:**
- Goblins are not considered a race in the way elves or orcs are; they are classified as "invasive fauna"
- Goblin land claims are dismissed as absurd ("They were squatting in holes. We built roads.")
- Imperial citizens in former goblin territories genuinely do not understand that they live on stolen land
- The cost of goblin suppression is enormous and growing; military quartermasters call it "the budget black hole"
- Some senior officials privately advocate simply withdrawing from the most problematic regions, but this is political suicide

**What humans say about goblins:**
- "Kill them when you see them. It's the law."
- "My uncle's farm lost three carts to goblin raiders this season. Pest control doesn't work."
- "They breed like rats. Clear one warren, two more pop up."
- "Some soft-hearted fool told me goblins have 'ancestral claims.' Ancestral claims? They're vermin."

### Gnomes: Mysterious and Coveted

**Official position:** "A reclusive island people with interesting technology. Trade is mutually beneficial."

**Actual position:** The gnomes possess technology that the empire desperately wants and cannot obtain by force. Their island isolation, protected by 280 kilometers of open ocean and unknown defensive capabilities, makes them unconquerable. The empire suspects the gnomes have airships, advanced automatons, and industrial capacity far beyond what they reveal, but cannot confirm it.

**The obsession:**
- Imperial intelligence devotes significant resources to understanding gnomish technology
- Gnomish trade goods are analyzed, reverse-engineered, and studied
- Gnomish traders are monitored and their conversations recorded
- The empire has twice attempted naval expeditions to the Gnomish Isles; neither fleet returned
- Some imperial scholars argue that gnomish automatons violate the spirit if not the letter of the magic ban

**What humans say about gnomes:**
- "Clever little things. Too clever for their own good."
- "What are they hiding on those islands? No one who goes there comes back."
- "A gnomish clockwork is worth its weight in gold. Where do they get the knowledge?"
- "No religion, no hierarchy, no private property. Sounds like dwarves but worse."
- "If we could get our hands on their airship designs..."

### Lizard Folk: Barely Acknowledged

**Official position:** "Desert dwellers of no significant consequence."

**Actual position:** The empire barely thinks about lizard folk. They are few in number, live in remote desert regions, and avoid imperial attention. Most humans have never met a lizard folk. Those who have encountered them describe them as useful guides and enigmatic traders.

**What the empire doesn't know:**
- The hidden river empire still exists beneath the desert
- Lizard folk founded the Veiled Hand
- Lizard folk lifespans of 600-800 years mean they personally remember events the empire has forgotten
- Lizard folk sects observe and document imperial behavior across centuries

**What humans say about lizard folk:**
- "Seen one once. Unsettling. Good at finding water, though."
- "They keep to themselves. Smart, given how the empire treats everyone who doesn't."
- "I heard they have cities under the sand. Probably just stories."

### Beast Folk: Vagrants and Thieves

**Official position:** "Itinerant peoples requiring documentation and monitoring."

**Actual position:** Beast folk, particularly cat folk, are the most scrutinized non-human population within imperial territory. Their nomadic lifestyle makes them difficult to track, their lack of permanent residence makes them suspicious, and their gambling culture makes them morally questionable in the eyes of the devout.

**The prejudice:**
- Beast folk are assumed to be thieves until proven otherwise
- Their fortune-telling is considered borderline heresy (implied magical ability)
- Their pattern recognition in gambling is attributed to "animal cunning" rather than intelligence
- Documentation requirements are strictest for beast folk; they must re-register in every city they enter
- When crimes occur in areas near beast folk encampments, they are investigated first regardless of evidence

**What humans say about beast folk:**
- "Lock your doors when the caravans come through."
- "Good entertainment, bad neighbors."
- "The cat folk read futures. Heresy, if you ask me. But I went to one once... just for fun."
- "They don't belong anywhere. That's what makes them dangerous."

## Internal Dissent

### The Cracks

Not all humans agree with the regime. The Holy Dominion is not a monolith of belief.

**Categories of dissent:**

| Type | Description | Estimated Size |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **The Uncomfortable** | Ordinary people who sense something is wrong but lack language or framework to articulate it. | 20-30% of population |
| **The Quiet Doubters** | Those who privately question Helios doctrine, racial hierarchy, or imperial policy. Never speak publicly. | 10-15% |
| **The Sympathizers** | Those who have positive relationships with non-humans and quietly extend help or kindness. | 5-10% |
| **The Active Dissidents** | Those who consciously oppose imperial policy through small acts of resistance. | 1-3% |
| **The Deserters** | Those who have left imperial territory for Shadow Fen, border regions, or orcish steppes. | <1% |

### What Dissent Looks Like

A farmer "accidentally" leaves food near known goblin territories and claims pest damage on the missing crops.

A merchant charges the same price to elven and human customers, pretending not to notice the law requires a surcharge.

On the western border, a soldier looks the other way when a beast folk family passes a checkpoint with expired papers.

A priest emphasizes Helios's compassion over His judgment and quietly omits the Doctrine of the Chosen from children's catechism.

Somewhere in the capital, a mother tells her children that orcs are people, not monsters, and prays they never repeat it in school.

A bureaucrat processes a non-human's documentation request promptly rather than letting it sit for months.

Small acts. Not rebellion. They do not threaten the regime. But they are real, they are constant, and the empire's ideological control is not as complete as it pretends.

### The Regime's Response

The empire does not fear large-scale human rebellion. It fears the slow erosion of belief.

**Countermeasures:**
- Regular reinforcement of doctrine through temple sermons (increased frequency during times of stress)
- Public executions of traitors and heretics to remind the population of consequences
- Informant networks that make dissent personally risky
- Victory Day celebrations that refresh the narrative of human superiority
- Controlled exposure to non-human populations (enough to maintain prejudice, not enough to build empathy)
- The Inquest's Shadow division monitors sentiment in major cities and reports trends to leadership

**The deepest fear:**
- That humans will simply stop believing. Not armed rebellion---just quiet disillusionment, spreading like damp through stone.
- An empire held together by faith collapses when faith dissolves.
- The cynical leadership knows this, which is why they maintain the religious facade even as they privately doubt everything it teaches.

## THE HELIOS REVELATION

### [CLASSIFICATION: ABSOLUTE --- ONE LIVING PERSON KNOWS THIS TRUTH]

---

### The Nature of Helios

Everything the Holy Dominion teaches about Helios rests on lies, half-truths, and one terrible secret.

**What the faithful believe:**
- Helios is a god
- Helios chose humanity
- Helios grants divine power to ordained priests
- Helios watches over the empire from His place in the heavens
- Helios will reward the faithful in eternal light after death

**The truth:**

Helios is not a god. He was never a god.

Helios is a demi-god --- a being of immense power, but not true divinity. Flesh and spirit and blinding light, possessing power far beyond mortal comprehension but lesser than the true gods who created the world.

And for five centuries, he has been imprisoned beneath the Holy City.

---

### The Chamber Beneath the Cathedral

Deep beneath the Grand Cathedral of Helios---far below the public worship halls, below the administrative offices, below the catacombs, below even the sealed archives---there exists a chamber that does not appear on any architectural plan.

The chamber is ancient. Older than the cathedral above it. Older than the empire. Older than human civilization. Carved by inhuman hands, in an age when the world was shaped differently.

Within this chamber, Helios was **kept**. Not worshipped. Kept.

**The arrangement:**
- Helios was bound to the chamber through means that predate human understanding
- His power radiated outward, sustaining the land, empowering human civilization, and fueling what the faithful call "divine magic"
- Not willing. Not free. A captive energy source.
- The earliest human leaders discovered him---or were led to him---and built their civilization on his imprisoned light
- Over centuries, they constructed theology around their prisoner: the god who chose them, the light that blessed them, the divine mandate that justified their dominion

**The Keeper:**
- Only ONE person per lifetime is permitted to enter the chamber
- The role is not inherited by blood but hand-picked by the current Keeper and passed to a chosen successor
- The Keeper maintains the chamber, performs rituals that sustain (or once sustained) Helios's binding, and carries the full weight of the truth
- Not the Emperor. Not the High Luminary. The Keeper may be anyone: a priest, a servant, a scholar, a soldier
- Most live unremarkable public lives while privately bearing the most devastating secret in the world
- If a Keeper dies before choosing a successor, the knowledge dies with them
- The Keeper's identity is unknown to all other living persons

**The succession:**
- When a Keeper grows old or ill, they select their successor based on criteria that have never been formally codified
- The new Keeper is brought to the chamber and shown the truth
- They are told the history as it was told to the previous Keeper, who was told by the one before, stretching back centuries
- Each telling introduces small variations; the earliest history is uncertain, shaped by generations of oral transmission between single individuals
- The new Keeper assumes the burden. The old Keeper dies, often shortly after, as though the purpose that sustained them has been fulfilled

---

### The Draining of Helios

Five hundred years ago, during the final phase of the Last World War, the empire made the decision that defines everything since.

**The situation:**
- The war was destroying everything. Magic wielded as mass destruction was erasing civilizations.
- The elven forces of Calidar, while not the aggressors, possessed magical capabilities that made them an existential threat
- The empire possessed Heaven's Atlas---an artifact of the gods---but lacked the power to activate it
- No mortal power source was sufficient. The Atlas required spiritual energy on a scale that only a divine being could provide.

**The decision:**
- The Keeper of that era was consulted (or coerced---accounts differ across transmissions)
- The Atlas was connected to Helios through rituals performed in the chamber
- When the Atlas was activated to destroy Calidar, it siphoned approximately 95% of Helios's remaining life force

**The result:**
- Calidar was destroyed. Forests vitrified. Rivers boiled. Millions died.
- The war ended.
- Helios was reduced to a husk --- not dead, but not alive. Suspended between death and life. A flicker where a bonfire once burned.
- The being that had powered human civilization for millennia was snuffed out in a single act of weaponized desperation

---

### The Current State of Helios

Helios lies in the chamber beneath the Grand Cathedral.

**His condition:**
- He does not speak
- He does not move
- He does not respond to prayer, ritual, or invocation
- A faint glow persists---so dim that the chamber is nearly dark
- The warmth that once radiated from him has faded to a temperature barely above the surrounding stone
- He is not dead. The Keeper can detect the faintest pulse of existence. But he is not alive in any way that matters.
- There is no word for what he is. Present but absent. Still there, but gone.

**What the leadership knows:**
- The Emperor and the High Luminary know that "Helios no longer answers." Successive Keepers shared fragments of the truth with power, in varying degrees of clarity.
- They do NOT know the full scope. Not that Helios is a demi-god. Not about the draining. Only that divine communion has ceased and the religious apparatus must be maintained regardless.
- Their response: maintain the facade. The empire depends on belief. If the faithful learned that Helios is silent, the theological foundation collapses, and the social order with it.

**What the Keeper knows:**
- Everything. The full history. The nature of Helios. The draining. The current state.
- The Keeper performs rituals in the chamber that accomplish nothing
- The Keeper maintains the appearance of divine communion for the benefit of the High Luminary's occasional inquiries
- The Keeper lives with the knowledge that the empire's entire religious foundation is built on a dying prisoner
- Every Keeper has wrestled with the same questions: Should I tell someone? Would revealing the truth save the world or destroy it? Is maintaining the lie a mercy or a crime?
- Every Keeper has chosen silence. So far.

---

### The Divine Magic Question

If Helios is a husk, why does "divine magic" still work?

Helios-ordained priests can still channel healing, blessing, purification, and holy combat magic. This magic is real. It functions. It has observable effects.

**Possible explanations (none confirmed):**

| Theory | Implication |
|--------|------------|
| **Residual energy** | Helios's 5% remaining life force still radiates enough power for mortal-scale magic, though it is slowly depleting |
| **Self-sustaining faith** | Belief itself generates power; the priests' conviction creates the energy they channel, independent of Helios |
| **External source** | Something else powers the magic---an unknown entity, a ley line, or ambient magical energy attributed to Helios but originating elsewhere |
| **Helios is recovering** | The demi-god is slowly regenerating, and the magic reflects his gradual return to consciousness |
| **The binding itself** | The ancient chamber generates power through its own mechanisms, and Helios was always incidental to the process |

The current Keeper does not know which explanation is correct. No Keeper has ever known. The magic works. The source is uncertain.

## HEAVEN'S ATLAS

### [CLASSIFICATION: HIGHEST --- KNOWN ONLY TO THE KEEPER AND FRAGMENTS TO SENIOR LEADERSHIP]

---

### The True Nature of the Atlas

Heaven's Atlas was not created by mortals. Not crafted by human hands, elven magic, or any civilization of the known world.

Heaven's Atlas was created by the gods themselves.

**Its intended purpose:**
- A tool to map reality, space, and time
- A cartographic instrument of divine scope, capable of charting the fabric of existence itself
- Secondarily, a weapon---because anything that can rewrite reality can destroy it

**Its function:**
- It operates as both philosopher's stone and weapon of mass destruction
- When activated, it can reshape geography, rewrite physical law within a targeted area, and annihilate matter at a fundamental level
- The destruction of Calidar was a fraction of its theoretical capability
- It was never meant for mortal hands

**The power requirement:**
- Activating the Atlas requires **massive amounts of spiritual energy or life force**
- No mortal power source is sufficient. Thousands of mages working in concert could not generate enough energy.
- When humans used it, they drained Helios because he was the only power source available
- Helios, being only a demi-god, was nearly destroyed by it
- A true god might have survived the activation intact. Helios barely survived at all.
- The Atlas was designed for gods: only beings of true divinity possess enough power to use it safely

**Current location:**
- Unknown. The empire officially claims it was destroyed after the war.
- The Keeper knows it was not destroyed. But the Keeper does not know where it is.
- Whether it remains intact, was shattered, was hidden, or was moved to a location beyond mortal reach is one of the great unsolved mysteries.
- If it still exists and another power source could be found, it could be used again.
- This keeps imperial leadership awake at night, even without knowing the full truth about Helios.

## THE ABSENCE OF THE GODS

### [CLASSIFICATION: DEEPEST MYSTERY --- NO ANSWERS EXIST]

---

### Where Are the Gods?

The gods who created Heaven's Atlas are gone.

Not a matter of faith or theology---observable fact. The true gods, the beings who shaped the world, created the races, forged artifacts of reality-altering power, are absent in a way they were not before.

**What is known:**
- Ancient records (preserved by lizard folk, elves, and dwarves) reference divine interventions that occurred regularly in ages past
- At some point before or during recorded history, these interventions ceased
- No true god has manifested, spoken, or acted in the known world within living memory of any race---and lizard folk remember 800 years, elves remember 10,000
- The cosmos feels empty in a way that the eldest living beings can perceive but not explain

**What is suspected:**
- The gods did not choose to leave. Something happened to them.
- Something terrible has gone wrong on a cosmic scale
- The creation of Heaven's Atlas itself may be related---was it a weapon they built to fight something? A tool to escape something? A last resort before their disappearance?

**The unanswerable questions:**

| Question | Status |
|----------|--------|
| Are the gods dead? | Unknown |
| Are the gods locked away somewhere? | Unknown |
| Did they leave voluntarily? | Unlikely---their artifacts suggest urgency, not choice |
| Do they know what happened to Helios? | Unknown |
| Did the Vel'sharath cult's dimensional breach have something to do with the gods' disappearance? | Possible but unconfirmed |
| Was Helios left behind because he was only a demi-god, too weak to escape whatever trapped the others? | Unprovable. But it fits. |
| If the gods returned, what would they think of what humans did to Helios? | No Keeper has wanted to answer this. |

### The Vel'sharath Connection

Ancient texts reference a cult called the **Vel'sharath** that attempted to breach the boundaries between dimensions. The timeline of their activities correlates loosely with the period during which divine interventions ceased.

**Possibilities:**
- The Vel'sharath breach damaged the dimensional barriers that allowed gods to interact with the mortal world
- The breach attracted something from outside that consumed or imprisoned the gods
- The gods sealed themselves away to prevent whatever the Vel'sharath unleashed from spreading
- The Vel'sharath succeeded in their goal, and their "success" was the gods' disappearance
- The correlation is coincidental and the gods' absence has an entirely different cause

No one knows. The Vel'sharath are believed extinct. Fragments of their records survive in the Calidar wastes, buried in ruins the empire would prefer stayed buried.

### Helios as the Forgotten Child

A theory held privately by several successive Keepers:

Helios was a demi-god---lesser than the true gods, powerful but not fully divine. When whatever event claimed the gods occurred, Helios was left behind. Too weak to be taken or to escape. Too small to matter in whatever cosmic catastrophe consumed his superiors.

He was left in his prison beneath the Holy City, alone, for millennia.

And then the humans found him. And built a religion on his captivity. And drained him to fuel a weapon.

The Keeper who first articulated this theory reportedly wept for three days.

## Key Lore Facts

2. **The Doctrine of the Chosen** teaches that Helios selected humanity above all other races. Foundational ideology, not subtle prejudice.

5. **The Luminary Inquest** is secret police with religious authority. Surveillance, detention, execution, and soul destruction are its tools.

10. Regional variations within the empire are significant. The capital is cosmopolitan; the borders are anxious; the southwest is sympathetic to Shadow Fen.

The system is administered by elven bureaucrats who know every weakness in the apparatus they maintain.
